# Sergo Jindariani

Sergo Jindariani is a senior scientist at the US Department of Energy's Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory (Fermilab), where he works on the [muon collider](../experiments/future-colliders.md) program.

## Key Contributions

Jindariani is one of the central advocates for a muon collider as the next major accelerator: "The nature of muons makes them perfect candidates for creating particle collisions that go beyond what's achievable at the LHC. A muon collider really seems like the right tool for understanding the questions that are facing particle physics now."

A muon collider would combine the clean collision environment of electron colliders (since [muons](../concepts/muon.md) are fundamental particles) with the high center-of-mass energies achievable only with more massive particles, potentially revealing [physics beyond the Standard Model](../open-questions/beyond-standard-model.md) including dark matter candidates and Higgs field dynamics.
