# Aram Apyan

Aram Apyan is an assistant professor at Brandeis University and member of the [ATLAS collaboration](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md) at CERN. His research focuses on using [W boson polarization](../concepts/w-boson-polarization.md) — particularly the longitudinal polarization state — as a precision probe of the [Higgs mechanism](../concepts/higgs-boson.md).

## W Boson Polarization Analysis

Apyan and his Brandeis colleagues (including postdoc Daniel Camarero Muñoz and graduate student Max Stange) developed a machine-learning-based analysis to identify longitudinally polarized W boson pairs in LHC collision data. The analysis is technically difficult because:

- W boson polarization cannot be measured directly — only inferred from decay products
- The relevant W boson decays involve [neutrinos](../concepts/neutrino.md), which leave no detector signature
- W boson *pairs* produce two neutrinos, creating directional ambiguity

Their approach uses boosted decision trees and deep neural networks trained with a multi-step bias-suppression validation system. In 2025, the analysis found evidence for W boson pairs with at least one longitudinally polarized member — a step toward probing WW scattering mediated by the Higgs.

Apyan's framing of the scientific goal: "We want to study longitudinally polarized W boson pairs because they can interact with each other through an intermediary Higgs boson. It's a really sensitive test of the Higgs mechanism."

On the ultimate prize: "This is one of the main processes that will eventually give us a complete understanding of the Higgs mechanism. There is a lot more work to do."
