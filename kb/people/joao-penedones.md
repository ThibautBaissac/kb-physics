# João Penedones

João Penedones is a physicist at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology Lausanne (EPFL). He studies the behavior of particles and quantum fields in [de Sitter space](../concepts/de-sitter-space.md).

## Key Contributions

### Massive Photons in De Sitter Space (2025)

In a paper posted to arxiv.org in May 2025, Penedones and his former student [Manuel Loparco](../people/manuel-loparco.md) investigated what a photon looks like in exponentially expanding space. Their calculation revealed a shocking result: massless photons could be composed of massive particles in de Sitter space. This implies that photons could spontaneously decay into matter, which could then decay back into light. Penedones says: "We're still trying to understand the physical implications of that."

### Energy Non-Conservation

Penedones has emphasized that energy is not conserved in de Sitter space: "The expansion is literally pumping energy, changing the universe." This is one of the fundamental ways in which physical intuition developed in flat space breaks down in an expanding universe.

### Value of De Sitter Studies

Penedones advocates studying de Sitter space to challenge flat-space assumptions: "De Sitter tells you that your intuition that you develop in flat space is not true in all spaces. That's why it's useful to do some things in de Sitter: to lose your prejudice."
