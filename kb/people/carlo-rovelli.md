# Carlo Rovelli

Carlo Rovelli is an Italian theoretical physicist at Aix-Marseille University. He is known for co-developing loop quantum gravity and for proposing **relational quantum mechanics** (RQM), an interpretation of quantum theory in which physical properties exist only relative to the interactions between systems.

## Relational Quantum Mechanics

Rovelli's interpretation, introduced in 1996, dissolves the [measurement problem](../open-questions/measurement-problem.md) by denying that quantum states describe absolute, observer-independent properties. Instead, the [wave function](../concepts/wave-function.md) of a system is always defined relative to another system (the observer).

When system A measures system B, A's state becomes correlated with B's — but this correlation is real only from A's perspective. A third system C may assign a completely different quantum state to the same situation. There is no contradiction because quantum states are not absolute facts about the world; they are facts about relations between systems.

This is closely related to [quantum entanglement](../concepts/quantum-entanglement.md): all interactions are entanglement events, and the relational perspective just insists that those entanglements don't pick out a preferred observer.

See [Relational Quantum Mechanics](../concepts/relational-quantum-mechanics.md) for the full conceptual account.

## Loop Quantum Gravity

Rovelli is also a pioneer of loop quantum gravity, a non-perturbative approach to quantizing general relativity without strings. In this framework, spacetime itself is granular at the Planck scale — area and volume come in discrete quanta. This is an alternative to string theory's approach to [UV completion](../concepts/uv-completion.md) of gravity.
