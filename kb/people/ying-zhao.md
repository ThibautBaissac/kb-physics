# Ying Zhao

Ying Zhao is a theoretical physicist at MIT who works on quantum gravity and the role of observers in closed universes.

## Closed Universe Paradox

As a collaborator of [Juan Maldacena](../people/juan-maldacena.md), Zhao was among the first to encounter the disturbing result that closed universes possess one-dimensional [Hilbert spaces](../concepts/hilbert-space.md) via the [island formula](../concepts/island-formula.md). She recalled being "pretty shocked by that argument" and initially attempted to refute it.

## Observer as Boundary

After moving to MIT in 2024, Zhao worked with [Daniel Harlow](../people/daniel-harlow.md) and Mykhaylo Usatyuk to develop [Edgar Shaghoulian](../people/edgar-shaghoulian.md)'s proposal that observers restore complexity. They conceptualized the observer as introducing a boundary — not around the universe itself, but around the observer. Their calculations demonstrated that when a classical observer inhabits a closed universe, the apparent complexity returns.

The MIT team published their findings in January 2025. Zhao remains cautious: "Am I really confident to say that it's right, it's _the_ thing that solves the problem? I cannot say that. We try our best."
