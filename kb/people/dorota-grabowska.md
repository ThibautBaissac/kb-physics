# Dorota Grabowska

Dorota Grabowska is a theoretical physicist at the University of Washington working on [quantum simulation](../concepts/quantum-simulation.md) and quantum algorithms for particle physics.

## Work

Grabowska's research sits at the intersection of quantum information and particle physics, focused on translating classical path-integral-based codes into quantum Hamiltonian formulations. She emphasizes the fundamental shift in thinking required: "To me it feels like a completely different way of thinking, which is exciting. It means that you're having to think about the same problems in completely different ways—and that is sometimes when breakthroughs happen."

She highlights the key challenge of decoherence and measurement: "The difficulty with quantum computers is you have to interact with them. But you can't interact with them too much, or else they stop being quantum." This is the central tension in quantum computation — extracting information necessarily disturbs the quantum state.

Grabowska sees the field at a turning point: "We are still quite limited in what we can do on quantum machines. But we're on the cusp of looking at the real-time properties of quantum systems that weren't accessible previously."
