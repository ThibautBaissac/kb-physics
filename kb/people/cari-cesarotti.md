# Cari Cesarotti

Cari Cesarotti is a postdoctoral fellow in the theory group at CERN. She grew up near Fermilab and was a high school junior when the Higgs boson was discovered in 2012.

## Key Views

### Particle Physics Isn't Dead

Cesarotti is a vocal advocate for particle physics despite the field's contraction: "Particle physics isn't dead; it's just hard." She warns that the brain drain is becoming self-fulfilling: "If you're pushing all these talented people out of trying to solve these problems into a field that it's easier to make an impact on, then you're setting yourself up for failure."

### The Hierarchy Problem Needs Humans

Cesarotti is skeptical of AI replacing theoretical physicists. She argues that chatbots make mistakes and have become "too much of a crutch for physics students." Her prescription: "What we need is humans to read textbooks and sit down and think of new solutions to the [hierarchy problem](../concepts/hierarchy-problem.md)."

### The Shrinking Field

Despite being a rising particle physicist, Cesarotti has yet to land a permanent position. She has watched the subfield shrink as hiring committees and graduate students move to other areas: "Definitely all this rhetoric that there was nothing to be found and you should give up on it — people listened."
