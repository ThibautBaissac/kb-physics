# Zohreh Davoudi

Zohreh Davoudi is a theoretical physicist at the University of Maryland working on the frontier of [quantum simulation](../concepts/quantum-simulation.md) for nuclear and particle physics.

## Work

Davoudi is among the leading researchers pushing toward "truly quantum simulations of nature" — simulating physical systems that classical computers fundamentally cannot handle. Her focus is on translating problems in [quantum chromodynamics](../concepts/quantum-chromodynamics.md) and nuclear physics from the classical path integral formulation into quantum (Hamiltonian) formulations suitable for quantum computers.

She has been involved in benchmarking quantum simulation across platforms, including studies of real-time string breaking — the QCD process whereby pulling apart strongly-bound quarks releases new particle-antiparticle pairs. The agreement in string-breaking mechanisms across multiple quantum platforms (analog and digital) provides cross-validation that quantum simulation is capturing real physics.

On the long-term outlook: "The progress we've seen in hardware over the past five to 10 years has been really breathtaking. I'm very hopeful that in 10 years, we'll be in a place that we could not predict today."
