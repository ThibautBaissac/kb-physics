# Christine Muschik

Christine Muschik is a theoretical physicist at the University of Waterloo in Canada, focused on developing quantum simulation algorithms for [quantum field theories](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md).

## Qudit Quantum Simulation

Muschik spent years using qubits to simulate quantum electrodynamics. After meeting [Martin Ringbauer](../people/martin-ringbauer.md) at the University of Innsbruck, she translated her algorithms into [qudit](../concepts/qudits.md) logic for his five-state ion-trap computer. The circuits shrank tenfold: "It was like putting them on a diet."

Together they achieved the first 2D [quantum simulation](../concepts/quantum-simulation.md) of particles and their quantum force field (*Nature Physics*, March 2025), using calcium-40 ion qudits. Their first 1D results were published in 2016.

## Vision

Muschik emphasizes the vast unknowns in [quantum chromodynamics](../concepts/quantum-chromodynamics.md): "In QCD, there's just an enormous amount of things we cannot calculate. Our lack of understanding is just so much more gigantic." She views qudit-based computers as the best path toward simulating QCD's full dynamics.
