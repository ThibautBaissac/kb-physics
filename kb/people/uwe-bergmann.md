# Uwe Bergmann

Uwe Bergmann is a physicist at the University of Wisconsin-Madison known for pioneering "X-ray fluorescence imaging" applications that extend particle physics detector technology into the humanities.

His most celebrated work used [SLAC's Stanford Synchrotron Radiation Lightsource](../experiments/slac-light-sources.md) to recover Archimedes' lost mathematical writings. A twelfth-century monk had scraped a parchment containing Archimedes' texts and reused it as a prayer book — a palimpsest. Bergmann's team detected the faint iron signature of the original ink using X-rays, making the ancient text legible without physically disturbing the manuscript.

The work demonstrates that [photon](../concepts/photon.md)-based techniques developed for physics research — tunable synchrotron X-rays, sensitive fluorescence detectors — have transformative applications in archaeology and the history of science.
