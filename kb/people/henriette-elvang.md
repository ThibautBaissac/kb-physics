# Henriette Elvang

Henriette Elvang is a physicist at the University of Michigan working on [scattering amplitudes](../concepts/scattering-amplitudes.md), [supersymmetry](../concepts/supersymmetry.md), and the [bootstrap program](../concepts/bootstrap-method.md).

## Key Contribution

In January 2026, Elvang published "String Theory From Maximal Supersymmetry," demonstrating that [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md) is the unique [UV completion](../concepts/uv-completion.md) for [quantum field theories](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md) with N=4 supersymmetry — the highest symmetry level.

The significance lies in the starting point: Elvang began purely from field theory assumptions without presupposing anything about the higher-energy theory. As she put it: "To really be able to say, 'I'm just using field theory; I'm not assuming anything about the higher-order theory,' and then string theory comes out of just field theory — that's really, really fun."

[Pedro Vieira](../people/pedro-vieira.md) noted that this result currently holds only for tree-level amplitudes; the full quantum extension remains open.
