# Pedro Vieira

Pedro Vieira is a physicist at the Perimeter Institute working on the [bootstrap program](../concepts/bootstrap-method.md) and [scattering amplitudes](../concepts/scattering-amplitudes.md).

## Perspectives

Vieira acknowledged recent bootstrap derivations of [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md) as "very nice work" but offered important caveats. He noted that [Henriette Elvang](../people/henriette-elvang.md)'s derivation holds only for **tree-level amplitudes** — the full quantum (loop-level) picture remains unresolved.

Vieira also highlighted an underappreciated development: extended objects — lines, surfaces — naturally arise within [quantum field theory](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md), which is traditionally formulated using point particles. He suggested: "Whether we should call these strings, I don't know. But understanding quantum theories requires thinking beyond just points." This observation blurs the traditional boundary between QFT and string theory.
