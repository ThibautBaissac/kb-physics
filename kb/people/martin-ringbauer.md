# Martin Ringbauer

Martin Ringbauer is an experimental physicist at the University of Innsbruck, Austria, who builds [qudit](../concepts/qudits.md)-based quantum computers for [quantum simulation](../concepts/quantum-simulation.md).

## Qudit Quantum Computer

Ringbauer's team built a quantum computer from calcium-40 ions, each with five accessible energy levels serving as quantum digits. This "beautiful machine" enabled a collaboration with [Christine Muschik](../people/christine-muschik.md) that produced the first 2D simulation of quantum electrodynamics (*Nature Physics*, March 2025).

The simulation used five ions — four at the corners and one at the center of a square lattice patch. "We have this building block ... and we can now just stack them next to each other to build up a big lattice," Ringbauer said.

## QCD Simulation

Ringbauer, [Jad Halimeh](../people/jad-halimeh.md), and collaborators have proposed a qudit algorithm for simulating hadron collisions — targeting [quantum chromodynamics](../concepts/quantum-chromodynamics.md) and the hadronization process in the early universe.
