# Stephen Hawking

<!-- stub: needs enrichment via kb:ingest -->

Stephen Hawking (1942–2018) was a British theoretical physicist whose work transformed our understanding of black holes and cosmology. His 1974 discovery that black holes emit thermal radiation ([Hawking radiation](../concepts/hawking-radiation.md)) connected general relativity, quantum mechanics, and thermodynamics for the first time. Together with Jacob Bekenstein's earlier work, this established [black hole entropy](../concepts/black-hole-entropy.md) and launched the [black hole information paradox](../open-questions/black-hole-information-paradox.md). Hawking and Bernard Carr also pioneered the theory of [primordial black holes](../concepts/primordial-black-holes.md) — black holes formed from density fluctuations in the early universe, now candidates for dark matter.
