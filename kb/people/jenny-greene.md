# Jenny Greene

Jenny Greene is a Professor of Astrophysical Sciences at Princeton University and a leading researcher on [Little Red Dots](../concepts/little-red-dots.md) — the anomalous compact objects discovered by the [James Webb Space Telescope](../experiments/james-webb-space-telescope.md) in the early universe.

Greene co-leads analysis of the RUBIES survey (Red Unknowns: Bright Infrared Extragalactic Survey), the primary dataset cataloguing LRDs. Her work centers on characterizing these objects' spectral and kinematic properties to distinguish between the AGN and stellar-density interpretations.

On the depth of the LRD puzzle: "Every time we think we understand something [about LRDs], they surprise us again by not having the properties we expect."

Her research connects to the broader open question of [supermassive black hole origins](../open-questions/supermassive-black-hole-origins.md) — specifically whether the extreme black hole-to-galaxy mass ratios in LRDs imply a reversed formation sequence compared to the modern universe.
