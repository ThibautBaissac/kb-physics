# Melissa Graham

Melissa Graham is a research scientist at the University of Washington and the lead community scientist for the [Vera C. Rubin Observatory](../experiments/vera-c-rubin-observatory.md). As a data-management science analyst, she works to prepare astronomers worldwide to analyze Rubin's massive alert stream — projected at approximately 10 million events per night once the Legacy Survey of Space and Time (LSST) is underway.

Graham has been studying [supernovae](../concepts/supernova.md) since her graduate thesis, and she will use Rubin's alerts to search for rare, scientifically distinctive supernovae: "We need a way to identify rare and unique events, the exemplars of their class that can provide particular physical insight." Supernovae serve as standard-candle distance markers central to probing [dark energy](../concepts/dark-energy.md).

She frames the core challenge of modern [time-domain astronomy](../concepts/time-domain-astronomy.md) as the "needle-in-a-haystack" problem: time-domain surveys now produce hundreds of events per night, far exceeding any individual researcher's capacity to process. Graham also serves as a liaison supporting the alert broker teams' development efforts, calling them "brilliant in the face of their challenges."
