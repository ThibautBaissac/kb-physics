# Jad Halimeh

Jad Halimeh is a physicist at the Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich, working at the intersection of analog and digital [quantum simulation](../concepts/quantum-simulation.md).

## Analog Quantum Simulation

In 2020, Halimeh and [Bing Yang](../people/bing-yang.md) published an analog simulation of quantum electrodynamics in one dimension using 71 rubidium atoms — a milestone for the analog approach. In December 2024, they used a rubidium-atom analog simulator to emulate the transition between bound and unbound quark states, probing how the strong force behaved during the universe's earliest moments.

## Digital Approaches

Halimeh also collaborates on digital [qudit](../concepts/qudits.md)-based approaches. With [Martin Ringbauer](../people/martin-ringbauer.md) and others, he proposed a qudit algorithm for simulating hadron collisions and hadronization in [quantum chromodynamics](../concepts/quantum-chromodynamics.md).

## On the Competition

Halimeh frames the central debate: "Now there's a competition. This is a big open question: What is the future, analog or digital?" He notes that quantum computers handle entanglement effortlessly: "Quantum computers deal with this like peanuts. It's extremely cheap."

He envisions large-scale quantum simulators enabling scans of any epoch in the early universe.
