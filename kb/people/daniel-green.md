# Daniel Green

Daniel Green is a cosmologist at the University of California, San Diego. His work focuses on the challenges of formulating quantum mechanics in [de Sitter space](../concepts/de-sitter-space.md).

## Key Contributions

Green has articulated several central difficulties of quantum theory in de Sitter space:

- **No safe haven from quantum fluctuations:** "Gravity is fluctuating, quantum mechanically, everywhere. And there's no place to shield yourself from that."
- **The observer problem:** "The whole machinery of quantum mechanics is built on the idea that there's a quantum system, and then some big, giant experimentalist comes along and measures that system." In de Sitter space, this separation between observer and system breaks down.
- **Asymptotic de Sitter future:** "People think eventually we will live in pure de Sitter space, or something like it, for a very long time."
- **Research strategy:** Green advocates sorting technical problems from conceptual ones to make progress — the hope is to be "in a much better position to solve some of these other bigger problems."

He has also suggested that the puzzling result of de Sitter space having no quantum states likely reflects a misinterpretation: "The most likely answer is that we're not interpreting that calculation correctly."
