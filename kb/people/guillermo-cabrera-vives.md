# Guillermo Cabrera-Vives

Guillermo Cabrera-Vives is a full professor at the University of Concepción in Chile and the leader of **ALeRCE** (Automatic Learning for the Rapid Classification of Events), one of six alert brokers that process the [Zwicky Transient Facility](../experiments/zwicky-transient-facility.md)'s nightly alert stream as a precursor to the [Vera C. Rubin Observatory](../experiments/vera-c-rubin-observatory.md).

ALeRCE grew out of Cabrera-Vives' work developing real-time machine-learning algorithms to detect [supernovae](../concepts/supernova.md). He and a colleague realized their detection system could be generalized into a full-service broker. The system is named after the alerce coniferous trees native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile and Argentina, known for their exceptional height.

Cabrera-Vives frames alerts as foundational to [time-domain astronomy](../concepts/time-domain-astronomy.md): "Alerts are at the heart of time-domain astronomy. Without them, we would miss many of the most exciting and short-lived phenomena in the universe." He also credits ZTF's precursor role: "ZTF has been a valuable precursor, helping the community prepare for the scale and speed of data Rubin will produce."

ALeRCE and Fink are among the six brokers slated to process Rubin's full alert stream of ~10 million events per night.
