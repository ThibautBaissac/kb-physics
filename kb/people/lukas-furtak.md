# Lukas Furtak

Lukas Furtak is an astronomer at Ben-Gurion University of the Negev, Israel.

## Discovery of QSO1

In 2023, Furtak first identified QSO1 while examining gravitational lens imagery from the [James Webb Space Telescope](../experiments/james-webb-space-telescope.md). The object appeared as three tiny red dots in the image, distinguishable from galaxies because they showed no signs of distortion despite the gravitational lensing effect.

Over six months, Furtak's team conducted spectroscopic analysis on each of the three lensed images, spending 40 hours on each observation. Their findings confirmed QSO1 as "a glowing black hole packing a mass of tens of millions of suns," existing when the universe was just 750 million years old.

[Roberto Maiolino](../people/roberto-maiolino.md) (Cambridge) later led follow-up observations that precisely measured the mass at 50 million solar masses and confirmed the absence of a parent galaxy — see [Supermassive Black Hole Origins](../open-questions/supermassive-black-hole-origins.md).
