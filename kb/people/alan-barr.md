# Alan Barr

Alan Barr is a physicist at the University of Oxford working at the intersection of particle physics and quantum information theory at the [Large Hadron Collider](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md).

## Key Contributions

- **LHC as quantum processor:** Barr frames the LHC as treating "the process of colliding things together and forming new particles as a quantum processor," where the two possible quark spin states correspond to the 0 and 1 states of a qubit
- **Measurement analogy:** He observed that when top quarks decay, the process is "mathematically an equivalent process to making a measurement" — connecting the decay of [entangled](../concepts/quantum-entanglement.md) top quarks to the [measurement problem](../open-questions/measurement-problem.md) and offering fresh perspective on the quantum-to-classical transition
