# John Donoghue

John Donoghue is a theoretical physicist at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst, and a recent winner of the J.J. Sakurai Prize for his contributions to [quantum field theory](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md). He is a leading figure in the revival of [quadratic gravity](../theories/quadratic-gravity.md).

## Key Contributions

### Ghost Unitarity

Working with Gabriel Menezes (University of São Paulo), Donoghue demonstrated that [ghost particles](../concepts/ghost-particles.md) in quadratic gravity do not actually violate unitarity in simple scenarios. The ghosts are so unstable that they vanish before they can destabilize the vacuum or produce negative probabilities: "Things everyone else would have said violated unitarity don't seem to."

He proposed that the real cost of ghosts is temporary micro-causality violation — ghost particles can briefly travel backward in time, with macroscopic time emerging as a statistical average over "temporally squishy micro-moments."

### Asymptotic Freedom of Gravity

Donoghue and collaborators discovered that quadratic gravity is [asymptotically free](../concepts/asymptotic-freedom.md) — gravity weakens at higher energies. Combined with [renormalizability](../concepts/renormalization.md), this suggests quadratic gravity could describe physics at arbitrarily small scales: "This could allow it to be the final theory," though he remains cautious: "I'm not convinced that this is the final theory."

### Starobinsky Inflation

Donoghue advocates Starobinsky inflation (based on quadratic gravity) as "the only remaining [inflation model] that makes sense from a quantum field theory point of view."
