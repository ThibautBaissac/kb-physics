# Chris Fuchs

Chris Fuchs is an American physicist at the University of Massachusetts Boston and the principal architect of **QBism** (Quantum Bayesianism), an interpretation of quantum mechanics that treats the quantum state as an agent's personal probability assignments rather than as an element of physical reality.

## QBism

Fuchs argues that quantum mechanics is best understood as "a user's manual for the world" — a tool that allows agents to update their beliefs as they interact with an unruly, unpredictable reality, rather than a description of what that reality fundamentally is.

In his framing, "a quantum state is a catalog of my degrees of belief about the consequences of my actions." The collapse of the wave function is not a physical event but a Bayesian update: an agent revises their beliefs in light of the measurement outcome they personally experience.

This sidesteps the [measurement problem](../open-questions/measurement-problem.md) by denying that the [wave function](../concepts/wave-function.md) describes a shared objective reality — it is always relative to a particular agent making particular choices. The theory is fundamentally first-person: no single God's-eye view of the universe exists.

See [QBism](../concepts/qbism.md) for the full conceptual account.

## At the Helgoland Centennial

Fuchs presented QBism at the June 2025 Helgoland conference marking 100 years of quantum mechanics. The gathering exposed deep disagreements among hundreds of physicists about what quantum theory means, with QBism among the positions defended.
