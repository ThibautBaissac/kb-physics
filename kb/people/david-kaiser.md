# David Kaiser

David Kaiser is a physicist and historian of science at MIT. He proposed that the record-breaking neutrino detected by [KM3NET](../experiments/km3net-neutrino-detection.md) in 2023 may have originated from an exploding [primordial black hole](../concepts/primordial-black-holes.md).

## Key Contributions

### PBH Origin Hypothesis (2025)

With doctoral student Alexandra Klipfel, Kaiser published an analysis in *Physical Review Letters* (September 2025) showing that a primordial black hole with initial asteroid mass, exploding ~2,000 AU away, could have produced the monster neutrino with ~8% probability. The final burst of [Hawking radiation](../concepts/hawking-radiation.md) would release all 17 Standard Model particles — "neutrinos and quarks and all kinds of exotic things."

Kaiser: "The numbers are outrageously congruent, considering I walked in half-joking."

### Detection via Mars Orbiters

Kaiser has proposed using spacecraft orbiting Mars to detect gravitational wobbles from nearby PBHs: "At any given time, there would be at least one PBH in the solar system," with one approaching Mars closely enough every 3–10 years to create measurable orbital changes.

### Historian of the Muon's Discovery

Kaiser has also written and spoken about the historical significance of the [muon](../concepts/muon.md)'s mid-1930s discovery. He contextualizes why it shocked the physics community: "This was an era where physicists thought they had the parts needed to explain what the world was made of. It took a long time for experimentalists and theorists to agree that there really was a need for a new particle." The muon's discovery catalyzed the expansion of the known particle catalog that ultimately produced the [Standard Model](../theories/standard-model.md).
