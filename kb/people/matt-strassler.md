# Matt Strassler

Matt Strassler is a particle physicist affiliated with Harvard University. He is known for advocating that the [LHC](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md) still has unexplored territory for discovering new physics at lower energies.

## Key Views

### Hidden Valleys

Strassler argues that while heavy novel particles would have already appeared as bumps in LHC data, lighter particles could hide in "hidden valleys" — subtle excesses in the data that require more careful analysis. For example, an unstable dark matter particle might occasionally decay into excessive muon-antimuon pairs. "There's a huge amount of unexplored territory there."

He rejects the view that all new physics must be at high energies: "For people who thought all the new physics is at high energies — they're very disappointed right now. I don't share that view. There are many opportunities for nature to provide clues at low energies."

### The End of an Easy Era

Strassler frames the current crisis in historical terms: "It was easy for 125 years. One thing led to the next. That lucky century has, for now, at least in the medium term, come to an end. That could change tomorrow, or next century, or who knows."

He is particularly excited about thorium-229 decay studies, which could reveal variations in fundamental constants.
