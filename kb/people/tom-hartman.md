# Tom Hartman

Tom Hartman is a physicist at Stanford University. He works on applying insights from black hole physics and [holography](../concepts/holography.md) to cosmological settings, particularly [de Sitter space](../concepts/de-sitter-space.md).

## Key Contributions

### Black Hole Cosmology

Hartman treats black hole physics as a stepping stone toward understanding quantum effects in cosmology: "We think of black hole cosmology as being sort of a warm-up problem for understanding quantum effects and cosmology. So anytime we make progress on black holes, we go back and ask: Can we apply this to de Sitter?"

### The Empty De Sitter Puzzle

When attempting to formulate a quantum theory of de Sitter space using holographic methods, Hartman found a puzzling result: "Like if you try to formulate a quantum theory of de Sitter space, there's some sense in which it wants to not have any states in it." This emptiness contrasts sharply with our particle-filled, increasingly de Sitter–looking universe.

### Holographic Optimism

Despite these difficulties, Hartman is increasingly confident that holography will eventually extend to de Sitter space: "That's always been kind of believed, but I think in the last few years, it's become more convincing."
