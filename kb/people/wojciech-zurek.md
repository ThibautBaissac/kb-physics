# Wojciech Zurek

Wojciech Zurek is a physicist at Los Alamos National Laboratory in New Mexico. He is one of the principal architects of [decoherence](../concepts/decoherence.md) theory and the creator of [quantum Darwinism](../concepts/quantum-darwinism.md).

## Key Contributions

### Decoherence (1970s–)

Working with H. Dieter Zeh starting in the 1970s, Zurek showed that [quantum entanglement](../concepts/quantum-entanglement.md) with the environment naturally destroys quantum superpositions — explaining why we don't observe quantum effects at macroscopic scales. This uses nothing beyond standard quantum mechanics.

### Quantum Darwinism (2000s–)

Zurek extended decoherence into a full framework explaining how classical reality emerges. He showed that certain quantum states ("pointer states") are robust enough to generate multiple identical imprints in the environment. These states are "selected" — by analogy with Darwinian evolution — for translation into the classical world because they survive environmental copying. He describes pointer states as "the fittest": "They can survive the process of copying, and so the information about them can multiply."

### The Epiontic Synthesis (2025)

In his 2025 book *Decoherence and Quantum Darwinism*, Zurek brought together decades of work into a grand synthesis. He argues the [wave function](../concepts/wave-function.md) is "epiontic" — both epistemic and ontic — reconciling the Copenhagen and many-worlds interpretations: "The two conflicting views of quantum states, [epistemic and ontic], and the insistence that states must be one or the other is wrong."

He introduced the term "extanton" for an observed object surrounded by identical environmental imprints — an element of "relatively objective existence" that constitutes our classical reality.
