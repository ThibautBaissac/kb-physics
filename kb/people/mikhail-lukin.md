# Mikhail Lukin

Mikhail Lukin is a physicist at Harvard University and a leader in quantum simulation efforts.

## On Classical Limits

Lukin articulates why classical computers fail at simulating quantum systems: the mathematical description of [entangled](../concepts/quantum-entanglement.md) particles grows exponentially. "At some point it exponentially explodes. You run out of memory on a classical computer."

## On the Field

Lukin sees the various approaches to [quantum simulation](../concepts/quantum-simulation.md) — digital qubits, digital [qudits](../concepts/qudits.md), analog cold-atom systems, and hybrids — as complementary: "They're all studying different aspects in a different way. It's quite an interesting time. But it's also quite early."
