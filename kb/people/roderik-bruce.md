# Roderik Bruce

Roderik Bruce is an accelerator physicist at CERN who led the beam diagnostics investigation during the [ALICE](../experiments/alice-detector.md) 2023 lead-ion run crisis.

When the ALICE inner tracker developed an anomalous "hole," Bruce's simulations identified lead-207 ion contamination as the cause. Lead-207 ions — produced when collimators at IR7 stripped a neutron from lead-208 beam particles — are 0.5% lighter than the intended beam, causing them to over-bend in the LHC's magnetic lattice and generate an oscillating secondary beam that overwhelmed ALICE's detector chips.

His team's solution — deliberately bumping the lead-207 secondary beam out of phase so it bypassed the ALICE collimator — reduced background by a factor of 100 and salvaged the run.
