# Alexssandre de Oliveira Jr.

Alexssandre de Oliveira Jr. is a Brazilian physicist working on [quantum thermodynamics](../concepts/quantum-thermodynamics.md).

## Thermometer for Quantumness

De Oliveira is the lead author of a 2025 paper (with [Patryk Lipka-Bartosik](../people/patryk-lipka-bartosik.md) and Jonatan Bohr Brask) proposing a non-destructive method for detecting [quantum entanglement](../concepts/quantum-entanglement.md) through thermodynamic measurements. The key insight is that a quantum memory, entangled with both a quantum system and a heat sink, can catalyze anomalous heat flow — converting entanglement within the quantum system into measurable energy in the sink, without disturbing the quantum state.

De Oliveira showed that Clausius' second law of thermodynamics represents the "classical limit" of a more complete quantum formulation. He is working with Roberto Serra's group at the Federal University of ABC (São Paulo) toward experimental realization using nuclear magnetic resonance in chloroform molecules.
