# Andrew Lucas

Andrew Lucas is a theoretical physicist at the University of Colorado, Boulder. He works on the theory of [electron hydrodynamics](../concepts/electron-hydrodynamics.md) and contributed calculations for the Columbia [supersonic electron experiment](../experiments/electron-fluid-experiments.md).

## Key Contributions

Lucas compares conventional electron transport to pinballs bouncing off bumpers — electrons ricochet off impurities in all directions, with only a slight average drift. He notes that Gurzhi's 1963 prediction of electron fluids was long dismissed because real wires were "full of dirt and impurities."

He sees electron hydrodynamics as a showcase for physics beyond existing frameworks: "It's a very appealing showcase of something that can't be explained in any textbook paradigm." The hope is that hydrodynamic descriptions will eventually help explain more enigmatic quantum systems "where electrons seem to melt away."
