# Hollis Akins

Hollis Akins is a PhD student at the University of Texas at Austin working on [Little Red Dots](../concepts/little-red-dots.md) — the class of compact, luminous early-universe objects discovered by the [James Webb Space Telescope](../experiments/james-webb-space-telescope.md).

Akins has been exploring the alternative, non-AGN interpretation: that LRDs are extraordinarily dense concentrations of ancient stars ("black hole stars") rather than actively accreting black holes. This model faces the challenge that the required stellar densities would exceed anything observed in the local universe by orders of magnitude — but it avoids the "missing X-ray" problem that afflicts the AGN interpretation.
