# Gregorio Carullo

Gregorio Carullo is a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Birmingham (formerly based in Copenhagen). He collaborated with [Vitor Cardoso](../people/vitor-cardoso.md) and Simon Maenaut to analyze 22 black hole merger events from the LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA network, placing the tightest constraints on the [no-hair conjecture](../concepts/no-hair-theorem.md) to date.

## Gravitational Wave Analysis

Carullo and Maenaut joined forces during the analysis: "We sort of jumped on a free desk and started coding together." Their combined dataset constrained deviations from Einstein's predictions to within 40 kilometers of black hole event horizons (arXiv:2411.17893, 2025).

On the prospects of next-generation [gravitational wave detectors](../experiments/gravitational-wave-detectors.md), Carullo is enthusiastic: "The Einstein Telescope will basically observe all the black holes in the universe of a certain mass range … which is incredible." The Einstein Telescope will push precision from 40 km down to football-field scales, potentially approaching the regime where quantum gravity effects near horizons might appear.
