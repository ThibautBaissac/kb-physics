# Matthew McCullough

Matthew McCullough is a physicist who has articulated an optimistic framework for interpreting the current era of null results in particle physics. In his view, failing to find expected particles like [supersymmetric](../concepts/supersymmetry.md) partners sends physicists "back to the drawing board in a way that winds up deepening our understanding" of physical reality.

This perspective echoes the historical arc of the 1887 Michelson-Morley experiment — a famous null result that demolished the luminiferous ether hypothesis but ultimately made space for Einstein's special relativity. The absence of evidence, McCullough argues, is itself evidence that compels deeper inquiry.
