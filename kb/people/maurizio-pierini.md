# Maurizio Pierini

Maurizio Pierini is a physicist at CERN working on LHC experiments. He has provided some of the most vivid language for the post-[supersymmetry](../concepts/supersymmetry.md)-null-result era. When the LHC failed to discover SUSY particles after the 2012 Higgs discovery, he described the experience as: "it's like we set out to win a 100-meter race only to find we were running a marathon."

The metaphor captures the consensus expectation — SUSY particles should appear right alongside the [Higgs boson](../concepts/higgs-boson.md) at ~125 GeV, making their discovery the next obvious step — and the shock of not finding them. Pierini characterizes the Higgs mass itself as "the smoking gun of new physics": it sits precisely where the [Standard Model](../theories/standard-model.md) should break down due to the [hierarchy problem](../concepts/hierarchy-problem.md), yet searches in surrounding energy ranges find only "smoke."
