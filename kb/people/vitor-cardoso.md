# Vitor Cardoso

Vitor Cardoso is a physicist at the Niels Bohr Institute in Copenhagen. He leads research on using gravitational wave observations to test general relativity and the [no-hair conjecture](../concepts/no-hair-theorem.md) — the prediction that black holes are fully characterized by only their mass and rotation.

## No-Hair Tests with Gravitational Waves

As the LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA network accumulated black hole merger events over a decade, Cardoso recognized the growing dataset as an opportunity for precision tests of general relativity in the strong-field regime: "As years went by and the events piled up, we realized that we could have stronger, more robust tests of the theory [of general relativity] — or alternatives."

Working with Gregorio Carullo and Simon Maenaut (and enabled by a 2023 KU Leuven technical breakthrough for fast-spinning black hole calculations), Cardoso's group analyzed 22 black hole merger events. Their 2025 analysis (arXiv:2411.17893) constrained any deviation from Einstein's predictions to within 40 kilometers of the event horizon at 95% confidence — the tightest such constraint to date.

The analysis focuses on the gravitational wave "ringdown" produced when two merged black holes settle into their final state. The ringdown's frequency and decay rate are exquisitely sensitive to the black hole's exterior structure, making it a precise probe of whether horizon-scale "hair" exists.
