# Sofia Vallecorsa

Sofia Vallecorsa is the coordinator of the Quantum Technology Initiative at CERN, working on applying quantum algorithms to analyze data from particle physics experiments.

## Work

Vallecorsa's work addresses a subtle challenge: particle collision processes at the [Large Hadron Collider](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md) are inherently quantum mechanical, but the data collected by classical detectors is not. The question is whether quantum computers can reconstruct the underlying quantum mechanical laws from classical detector data.

Her results are encouraging: "The good news is that they can, and for the different tasks that we have tried quantum algorithms on so far, we prove that they actually have good performance." This opens the possibility that quantum algorithms could recover quantum signatures from experimental data in ways classical analysis cannot.

This work is part of [CERN's broader quantum computing initiative](../concepts/quantum-simulation.md), exploring whether quantum hardware can give particle physics a computational edge beyond what classical machines provide.
