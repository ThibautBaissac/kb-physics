# Edgar Shaghoulian

Edgar Shaghoulian is a physicist at UC Santa Cruz working on quantum gravity and the role of observers in closed universes.

## Key Contribution: Observer Dependence

In 2023, Shaghoulian noticed that topological field theories — mathematical frameworks used to study geometric shapes — exhibit the same one-dimensional [Hilbert space](../concepts/hilbert-space.md) problem as closed universes. However, when subdividing these spaces into zones, the Hilbert space enlarges: "The rules of the game change."

Drawing on this analogy, Shaghoulian proposed that introducing an observer into a closed universe would similarly expand its Hilbert space, resolving the paradox discovered by [Maldacena](../people/juan-maldacena.md) using the [island formula](../concepts/island-formula.md). This idea was subsequently developed by [Ying Zhao](../people/ying-zhao.md), [Daniel Harlow](../people/daniel-harlow.md), and Mykhaylo Usatyuk at MIT.
