# Adam Falkowski

Adam Falkowski is a Paris-based particle physicist, formerly known for sharp commentary on his blog *Résonaances* (inactive since 2022).

## Key Views

### The 2012 Prediction

After the LHC found no new physics beyond the Higgs, Falkowski predicted the field would undergo "a slow decay": "The number of jobs in particle physics will steadily decrease, and particle physicists will die out naturally."

### Skepticism About Future Colliders

Falkowski remains skeptical: "I am very skeptical about future colliders. For me it's very difficult to get excited about it." He worries about the huge costs and the fact that "there are absolutely no hints that something is there within the reach of the next collider."

### Turn to Amplitudes

Falkowski has pivoted to the theoretical study of [scattering amplitudes](../concepts/scattering-amplitudes.md) — "amplitudeology" — which he sees as the most productive frontier: "There is a very vibrant program in trying to understand the structure of the physical theories. The hope is that with the help of machine learning, that there can be very fast progress in the coming years. I think that's where the best things have happened."

He believes experimental particle physics is dying, watching talented postdocs leave for data science and AI.
