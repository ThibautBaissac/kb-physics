# Regina Demina

Regina Demina is a physicist at the University of Rochester who proposed and led the CMS experiment's measurement of quantum [magic states](../concepts/magic-states.md) in entangled top quark pairs at the [Large Hadron Collider](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md).

## Key Contributions

- **Magic at the LHC:** After meeting [Martin](../people/martin-white.md) and [Chris White](../people/chris-white.md) at a conference, Demina was inspired to propose the magic measurement to her CMS group, leading to the first detection of quantum magic in particle physics
- **Toponium discovery:** The sensitive measurement methods developed for magic detection also revealed [toponium](../concepts/toponium.md), a previously undetected bound state
- **Page-Wootters at the LHC:** Demina has proposed testing the [Page-Wootters mechanism](../concepts/page-wootters-mechanism.md) — the idea that time is an emergent property of entanglement — using elementary particles at the LHC

Demina described the convergence of particle physics and quantum information as "really an emergent field" and "like a gold rush right now."
