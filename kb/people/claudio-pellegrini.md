# Claudio Pellegrini

Claudio Pellegrini is a distinguished professor emeritus at UCLA and a pioneer of free-electron laser (FEL) science. He played a central role in the development of the Linac Coherent Light Source (LCLS) at SLAC — the world's first hard X-ray free-electron laser — and has long championed the scientific potential of [photon](../concepts/photon.md)-based research facilities.

Pellegrini on the centrality of photons: "Photons have always been key to how we observe and comprehend nature." He further notes that "photons have demonstrated quantum mechanics's strangest characteristics," with recent LCLS collaborations exploiting quantum properties of X-ray light.

His work bridges accelerator physics and the broader scientific community that relies on [SLAC's light sources](../experiments/slac-light-sources.md) for molecular imaging, materials science, and quantum X-ray science.
