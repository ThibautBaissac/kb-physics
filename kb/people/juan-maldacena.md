# Juan Maldacena

Juan Maldacena is a theoretical physicist at the Institute for Advanced Study (IAS) in Princeton. He is one of the most influential figures in modern theoretical physics.

## AdS/CFT Correspondence

In 1997, Maldacena demonstrated that [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md) calculations could be simplified using [anti-de Sitter](../concepts/anti-de-sitter-space.md) geometry — a universe with boundaries resembling a tin can. Within this framework, everything occurring inside the can appears as shadows on its outer boundary, like a 3D projection onto a flat screen. This AdS/CFT correspondence became the most developed realization of [holography](../concepts/holography.md) and remains one of the most cited results in theoretical physics.

## Island Formula and Closed Universes

In 2019, Maldacena and colleagues developed the [island formula](../concepts/island-formula.md), a tool that tracks regions within black holes and helped explain how black holes can release information despite their gravitational barriers.

When he applied the island formula to closed universes — spacetimes without boundaries — Maldacena discovered that they possess one-dimensional [Hilbert spaces](../concepts/hilbert-space.md), containing zero information. This startling result launched a research program exploring whether observers are essential to the complexity of closed universes.
