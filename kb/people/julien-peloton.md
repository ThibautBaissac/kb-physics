# Julien Peloton

Julien Peloton is a research engineer at the Irène Joliot-Curie Physics of the two Infinities Laboratory (IJCLab) in France and leads the technical team at **Fink**, a general-purpose [time-domain astronomy](../concepts/time-domain-astronomy.md) alert broker. Fink is one of six brokers processing the [Zwicky Transient Facility](../experiments/zwicky-transient-facility.md)'s alert stream as a testbed for the [Vera C. Rubin Observatory](../experiments/vera-c-rubin-observatory.md), and is slated to process Rubin's full stream of ~10 million nightly alerts.

Peloton emphasizes that machine learning is central to Fink's approach — different broker teams experiment with different strategies for classifying transient events. He views community engagement as equally important as technical excellence: "For me, the biggest difficulty is always to make sure that what I develop and deploy is useful. I spend a lot of time reaching out to the community to understand their needs."

He also stresses collaboration between broker teams: "Broker teams cooperate a lot to avoid reinventing the wheel and make sure we're all going in the right direction."
