# Cory Dean

Cory Dean is a physicist at Columbia University who specializes in [graphene](../concepts/graphene.md) experiments and [electron hydrodynamics](../concepts/electron-hydrodynamics.md).

## Key Contributions

### Supersonic Electron Fluid (2025)

Dean's lab, with postdoc Johannes Geurs, produced the most dramatic demonstration of electron fluid behavior to date. By carving bilayer graphene into a de Laval nozzle, they accelerated an electron fluid past its sound barrier, producing shock waves — the electron equivalent of a sonic boom. See [electron fluid experiments](../experiments/electron-fluid-experiments.md).

Dean emphasizes the contrast between electron and water flow: "Water is seeing nothing but other water, but in an electronic system, in a wire, that's manifestly not the case." Graphene changes this because it is "a thermodynamically beautiful crystal" with "very few impurities."
