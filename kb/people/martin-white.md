# Martin White

Martin White is a physicist at the University of Adelaide who, together with his identical twin brother [Chris White](../people/chris-white.md) (Queen Mary University of London), proposed that the [Large Hadron Collider](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md) could detect quantum [magic](../concepts/magic-states.md) in entangled top quark pairs.

## Key Contributions

- **Magic at the LHC:** Co-proposed the theoretical framework for measuring magic in top-antitop quark entanglement, recognizing that the LHC — itself a quantum system — could reveal this quantum information property
- **Inspiring CMS measurement:** Their work inspired [Regina Demina](../people/regina-demina.md) to propose the measurement to the CMS experiment

On the collaboration with his brother: "I found it really quite emotional when it was released. We wanted to work together for many years."

On the field's evolution: "There is a sense that you're always looking for new things to do."
