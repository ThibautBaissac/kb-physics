# Ben Rusholme

Ben Rusholme is chief engineer and senior scientist at the IPAC Science & Data Center for Astrophysics and Planetary Sciences at Caltech. He works on the [Zwicky Transient Facility](../experiments/zwicky-transient-facility.md), the northern-sky time-domain survey that has been issuing ~200,000 nightly alerts since 2018.

Rusholme describes ZTF's core method — comparing images of the same sky patch taken at different times — as "a sophisticated game of spot the difference." He emphasizes that the current era of large-scale systematic astronomy requires a collaborative ecosystem: surveys, alert brokers, and researchers each contributing specialized expertise. "Astronomy is changing to large-scale systematic observation that requires experts with different skills. It's no longer a single scientist working alone. It requires a village."

ZTF, under Rusholme's stewardship, has served as the principal proof-of-concept for the broker infrastructure that the [Vera C. Rubin Observatory](../experiments/vera-c-rubin-observatory.md) will depend on to process its ~10 million nightly alerts.
