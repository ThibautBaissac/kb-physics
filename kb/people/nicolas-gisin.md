# Nicolas Gisin

Nicolas Gisin is a physicist at Constructor University and the University of Geneva. He is a leading figure in quantum information and quantum foundations.

## Complex Numbers and Quantum Mechanics

In 2021, Gisin and colleagues designed a groundbreaking experimental protocol to test whether [complex numbers](../concepts/complex-numbers-in-quantum-mechanics.md) are essential to quantum mechanics or merely a mathematical convenience. Their insight was to extend the standard Bell test — normally involving two [entangled](../concepts/quantum-entanglement.md) particles and two participants (Alice and Bob) — to a more complex setup with two separate sources of entangled particles and three participants (Alice, Bob, and Charlie).

They showed that real-valued and complex-valued quantum theories predict different ceilings on entanglement correlations in this extended setup. A team at USTC subsequently ran the experiment and found correlations exceeding the real-valued limit, initially suggesting complex numbers are essential.

However, in 2025, three independent groups overturned this conclusion by showing that Gisin's protocol assumed a specific form of the tensor product that is not the only valid choice for real-valued theories. With generalized tensor products, real-valued quantum theories reproduce all predictions of the complex theory.
