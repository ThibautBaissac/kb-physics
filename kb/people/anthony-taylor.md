# Anthony Taylor

Anthony Taylor is a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Texas at Austin working on [Little Red Dots](../concepts/little-red-dots.md) — the anomalous compact objects found by the [James Webb Space Telescope](../experiments/james-webb-space-telescope.md) in the early universe.

Taylor led the kinematic analysis of the RUBIES survey (Red Unknowns: Bright Infrared Extragalactic Survey), demonstrating in a January 2025 paper in *The Astrophysical Journal* that over 70% of examined LRDs display rapidly rotating gas — the kinematic hallmark of material accreting onto a supermassive black hole. This finding is one of the strongest pieces of evidence that at least some LRDs host AGN, though the absence of expected X-ray emission remains unexplained.
