# Bing Yang

Bing Yang is an experimental physicist based in Shenzhen, China, specializing in analog [quantum simulation](../concepts/quantum-simulation.md) using ultracold atoms.

## Analog QED Simulation

In 2020, Yang and [Jad Halimeh](../people/jad-halimeh.md) published an analog simulation of quantum electrodynamics in one dimension using an array of 71 rubidium atoms — a milestone for the analog approach.

## Strong Force Simulations

In December 2024, Yang, Halimeh, and collaborators used a rubidium-atom analog simulator to emulate the transition between bound and unbound quark states, probing how the strong force in [quantum chromodynamics](../concepts/quantum-chromodynamics.md) might have behaved during the universe's earliest moments (quark-gluon plasma era).

Yang advocates for analog simulators when dealing with large particle counts typical of quark-gluon interactions: "You can go to really large systems."
