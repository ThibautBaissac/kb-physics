# Roberto Maiolino

Roberto Maiolino is an astrophysicist at the University of Cambridge.

## QSO1 Characterization

In 2024, Maiolino led follow-up observations of QSO1 using the [James Webb Space Telescope](../experiments/james-webb-space-telescope.md), building on the initial discovery by [Lukas Furtak](../people/lukas-furtak.md). His team spent 10 hours on additional JWST observations, achieving pixel-level spectral analysis that measured gas movement around the black hole.

Key findings:
- **Mass:** 50 million solar masses, directly calculated from gas orbital velocities
- **No parent galaxy:** The gas orbits the central point much like Earth orbits the sun, indicating matter compressed into an incredibly dense form
- **Composition:** At least two-thirds of QSO1's total mass is the black hole itself, with the rest being gas and possibly some stars
- **Primordial gas:** The orbiting material is essentially pure hydrogen — unchanged since the Big Bang — suggesting the black hole formed before nearby stars had scattered heavier elements

These results made QSO1 a key piece of evidence in the debate over [supermassive black hole origins](../open-questions/supermassive-black-hole-origins.md).
