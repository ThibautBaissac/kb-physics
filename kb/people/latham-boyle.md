# Latham Boyle

Latham Boyle is a physicist at the University of Edinburgh.

## Perspectives on String Theory

Boyle offers a nuanced position in the [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md) debate. He does not believe the bootstrap results necessarily imply string theory is true, but recognizes they reveal something mathematically exceptional about it:

"I don't think that the most likely implication is that string theory has to be true. But these results do show that there's something very special about string theory."

Boyle groups string theory with other exceptional mathematical objects — Penrose tilings, quaternions — that repeatedly emerge across mathematics and physics, suggesting it occupies a privileged structural role regardless of its physical truth.

He also questioned whether [scattering amplitudes](../concepts/scattering-amplitudes.md) are the right framework for the universe's highest energies, raising a foundational challenge to the [bootstrap program](../concepts/bootstrap-method.md).

More broadly, Boyle observed: "The laws of physics have gotten more mathematically beautiful as we've understood them better."
