# Kellogg Stelle

Kellogg Stelle was a theoretical physicist at Imperial College London who, as a graduate student at Brandeis University in the mid-1970s, discovered [quadratic gravity](../theories/quadratic-gravity.md) — the unique [renormalizable](../concepts/renormalization.md) quantum field theory of gravity (published 1977). He died in late 2025.

## Key Contribution

Stelle found that adding two curvature-squared terms to Einstein's equation makes gravity renormalizable, allowing it to be quantized using standard [quantum field theory](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md) techniques. However, the theory introduces a [ghost particle](../concepts/ghost-particles.md) with negative energy — a problem Stelle himself recognized: "I was also aware that not everything was going to be hunky-dory about this."

After supergravity emerged shortly after his 1977 paper, Stelle moved on and never returned to quadratic gravity himself, though he watched its recent revival from afar, calling it "agreeable." He viewed the theory as "a possible link, a kind of intermediate regime" rather than a final theory of gravity.

His original paper, long receiving only 10–20 citations per year, has recently been accruing more than 150 citations annually.
