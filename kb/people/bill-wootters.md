# Bill Wootters

Bill Wootters is a quantum information theorist at Williams College. He is a pioneer of quantum information theory and has made foundational contributions to understanding entanglement, quantum teleportation, and the mathematical structure of quantum mechanics.

## Complex Numbers in Quantum Theory

Wootters has been a key voice in the debate over whether [complex numbers](../concepts/complex-numbers-in-quantum-mechanics.md) are essential to quantum mechanics. He observed that "Quantum theory really is the first physical theory where the complex numbers seem to be right smack in the middle of the theory" — distinguishing it from other fields where complex numbers are merely convenient.

He noted that early real-valued quantum theories could reproduce standard Bell test results: "For a lot of things, you actually can get away with the real theory." Yet even after 2025 real-valued reformulations eliminated explicit use of *i*, Wootters pointed out that the hallmarks of complex-number arithmetic persist: "Even when you translate quantum theory into real numbers, you still see the hallmark of complex-number arithmetic."
