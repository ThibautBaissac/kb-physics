# String Theory

String theory proposes that at extraordinarily small scales, the fundamental constituents of matter are not point particles but vibrating one-dimensional strings. The theory requires ten spacetime dimensions — the four we observe plus six [extra dimensions](../concepts/extra-dimensions.md) curled up at undetectably small scales.

Emerging 58 years ago (circa 1968), string theory remains the leading candidate for a unified mathematical framework describing all matter and forces, though this status is highly contested.

## Key Results

### Anomaly Cancellation (1984)

John Schwarz and Michael Green showed that string theory's internal structure automatically cancels problematic mathematical anomalies that plague other theories attempting to unify gravity with quantum mechanics. This launched the "first superstring revolution."

### Black Hole Entropy (1996)

Andrew Strominger and Cumrun Vafa used string theory to derive [black hole entropy](../concepts/black-hole-entropy.md), recovering the Hawking-Bekenstein formula through microscopic state-counting. This provided the first statistical mechanical derivation of black hole thermodynamics.

## The Bootstrap Approach

A new line of research uses the [bootstrap method](../concepts/bootstrap-method.md) to derive string theory from physical principles rather than assuming it. Two key papers:

- **"Strings From Almost Nothing" (2025):** [Cliff Cheung](../people/cliff-cheung.md) and collaborators showed that assuming [ultrasoftness](../concepts/ultrasoftness.md) uniquely yields string theory's [scattering amplitudes](../concepts/scattering-amplitudes.md).
- **"String Theory From Maximal Supersymmetry" (2026):** [Henriette Elvang](../people/henriette-elvang.md) demonstrated that string theory is the unique [UV completion](../concepts/uv-completion.md) for theories with N=4 [supersymmetry](../concepts/supersymmetry.md).

As Cheung put it: "People have studied for decades what string theory implies, and we're asking, 'What implies string theory?'"

## Criticisms

The theory faces two central objections:

1. **Untestability:** The string-scale substructure exists at energies far beyond any foreseeable experiment. See [testability of string theory](../open-questions/testability-of-string-theory.md).
2. **The landscape problem:** The [string landscape](../concepts/string-landscape.md) permits at least 10^500 different configurations, making it unclear how to identify our universe's specific solution.

[Latham Boyle](../people/latham-boyle.md) offered a nuanced view: "I don't think that the most likely implication is that string theory has to be true. But these results do show that there's something very special about string theory." He compared it to exceptional mathematical objects like Penrose tilings and quaternions that recur across mathematics and physics.

## Extended Objects

[Pedro Vieira](../people/pedro-vieira.md) noted that extended objects — lines, surfaces — naturally arise within [quantum field theory](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md), suggesting the boundary between string theory and field theory may be less sharp than traditionally assumed.

## Quadratic Gravity as Alternative

[Quadratic gravity](../theories/quadratic-gravity.md) presents a competing approach to quantum gravity that stays entirely within [quantum field theory](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md). Interest in quadratic gravity has grown as string theory failed to deliver promised breakthroughs and superpartners failed to materialize at the LHC. If quadratic gravity's spacetime continuum holds to arbitrary scales, it would undermine the widespread belief — supported by string theory and black hole physics — that spacetime unravels into stranger substructure at submicroscopic scales.

## The De Sitter Problem

A major challenge has been formulating string theory in [de Sitter space](../concepts/de-sitter-space.md) — the expanding geometry that most resembles our universe. The best-understood string theory models describe universes with negative or zero energy ([anti-de Sitter](../concepts/anti-de-sitter-space.md) or flat), while our universe has positive dark energy.

### First Explicit De Sitter Solution (2025)

[Bruno Bento](../people/bruno-bento.md) and [Miguel Montero](../people/miguel-montero.md) (Madrid) derived the first explicit de Sitter solution from string theory, using a Casimir-like mechanism in [compactified](../concepts/compactification.md) [extra dimensions](../concepts/extra-dimensions.md). Thomas Van Riet (KU Leuven): "It is the very first example [from string theory] of an explicit de Sitter space." Antonio Padilla (Nottingham): they "open up a new frontier to finding explicit de Sitter solutions in string theory."

Key caveats: the solution is 5D (not 4D), the dark energy is dynamical (weakening over time), and the computed value (10^-15 Planck units) is far from the observed 10^-120. The dynamical dark energy matches preliminary DESI observations of weakening dark energy.

See also [quantum gravity in de Sitter space](../open-questions/quantum-gravity-in-de-sitter-space.md) for the broader open problem.
