# Standard Model

The Standard Model of particle physics is a [quantum field theory](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md) governing the 25 known elementary particles and their interactions via three of the four fundamental forces (electromagnetic, weak, and strong — but not gravity). Developed in the 1970s, it was completed with the discovery of the Higgs boson at the [Large Hadron Collider](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md) in 2012.

## Force Carriers

The Standard Model's force carriers are bosons. The [photon](../concepts/photon.md) mediates electromagnetism — massless, chargeless, it is the particle of light and of all electromagnetic radiation. The W and Z bosons carry the weak force; gluons carry the strong force; and the Higgs boson gives mass to other particles.

## A Surprise That Expanded the Catalog

The road to the Standard Model passed through many unexpected discoveries. The [muon](../concepts/muon.md), found in cosmic ray tracks in the mid-1930s, was the first particle nobody asked for — Nobel laureate Isidor Isaac Rabi quipped, **"Who ordered that?"** Its discovery forced physicists to abandon the assumption they had already found nature's complete inventory, opening the door to the particle zoo that the Standard Model eventually organized.

## The Higgs Boson and Its Couplings

The [Higgs boson](../concepts/higgs-boson.md) — the last Standard Model particle, discovered at the LHC in 2012 — couples to other particles in proportion to their mass. Confirming this pattern across all three generations of quarks and leptons is a key precision goal. The decay to bottom quarks (the dominant channel, ~58%) was observed in 2018; measuring the coupling to second-generation **charm quarks** (~3%) was long considered undetectable at the LHC. Machine-learning advances by [Huilin Qu](../people/huilin-qu.md) and [Loukas Gouskos](../people/loukas-gouskos.md) (CMS) improved sensitivity limits by four orders of magnitude, bringing this measurement within reach of the High-Luminosity LHC.

## Completeness and Precision

Every prediction of the Standard Model has been confirmed experimentally, with increasing precision as AI-assisted data analysis improves [scattering amplitude](../concepts/scattering-amplitudes.md) measurements at the LHC. Michelangelo Mangano (CERN) notes the collider now serves as a tool for exploring the Standard Model's predictions, since "not all consequences of the equations are easy to calculate."

## What It Doesn't Explain

Despite its success, the Standard Model leaves fundamental questions unanswered:

- **Dark matter:** No Standard Model particle is a dark matter candidate
- **Matter-antimatter asymmetry:** Why matter dominates over antimatter in the universe
- **The Big Bang:** No explanation for the origin of the universe
- **The [hierarchy problem](../concepts/hierarchy-problem.md):** The enormous, seemingly unnatural gap between the Higgs mass and the Planck scale
- **Gravity:** The Standard Model does not incorporate general relativity

These gaps motivate the search for [physics beyond the Standard Model](../open-questions/beyond-standard-model.md).
