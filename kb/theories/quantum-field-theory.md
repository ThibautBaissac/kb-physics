# Quantum Field Theory

Quantum field theory (QFT) is the mathematical framework that combines quantum mechanics with special relativity. It is traditionally formulated in terms of point particles and their interactions, and underpins the Standard Model of particle physics.

## Relationship to String Theory

QFT and [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md) have a complex relationship. String theory can be seen as a [UV completion](../concepts/uv-completion.md) of certain quantum field theories — resolving the infinities that arise at high energies.

[Henriette Elvang](../people/henriette-elvang.md) demonstrated that starting purely from QFT assumptions with N=4 [supersymmetry](../concepts/supersymmetry.md), string theory emerges as the unique UV completion: "To really be able to say, 'I'm just using field theory; I'm not assuming anything about the higher-order theory,' and then string theory comes out of just field theory — that's really, really fun."

## Extended Objects

[Pedro Vieira](../people/pedro-vieira.md) highlighted that extended objects — lines and surfaces — naturally arise within QFT even though the theory is formulated using point particles. He suggested that "understanding quantum theories requires thinking beyond just points," blurring the traditional distinction between field theory and [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md).

## QFT in De Sitter Space

Quantum field theory faces profound difficulties in [de Sitter space](../concepts/de-sitter-space.md). Unlike flat or [anti-de Sitter space](../concepts/anti-de-sitter-space.md), de Sitter space has no accessible boundary where quantum fluctuations vanish, making standard QFT observables ill-defined. Key breakdowns include:

- Energy is not conserved — the expansion pumps energy into the universe
- The concept of a stable, localized particle breaks down
- Massless photons can be composed of massive particles (Penedones & Loparco, 2025), upending standard notions of particle stability

These problems are central to the open question of [quantum gravity in de Sitter space](../open-questions/quantum-gravity-in-de-sitter-space.md).

## Renormalization and Gravity

A central challenge of QFT is [renormalization](../concepts/renormalization.md) — the procedure for absorbing infinite sums over field ripples into measurable constants. Electromagnetism is renormalizable: small ripples have limited impact. Gravity (general relativity) is non-renormalizable: the tinier the spacetime ripples, the more they matter, resisting any blurring.

[Quadratic gravity](../theories/quadratic-gravity.md) resolves this by adding curvature-squared terms to Einstein's equation, making gravity behave more like electromagnetism. It is the unique renormalizable QFT of gravity, though it introduces [ghost particles](../concepts/ghost-particles.md) — negative-energy excitations whose physical consistency remains debated.

## Quantum Simulation of QFTs

Simulating the real-time behavior of quantum fields is one of the key motivations for building quantum computers. Classical computers fail because [entanglement](../concepts/quantum-entanglement.md) between field excitations causes exponential growth in computational complexity. [Quantum simulation](../concepts/quantum-simulation.md) approaches — using digital [qudit](../concepts/qudits.md) computers or analog cold-atom systems — have achieved 2D simulations of quantum electrodynamics (2025). The ultimate goal is simulating [quantum chromodynamics](../concepts/quantum-chromodynamics.md) to understand the strong force in extreme conditions.
