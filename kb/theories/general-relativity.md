# General Relativity

<!-- stub: needs enrichment via kb:ingest -->

Einstein's general theory of relativity (1915) describes gravity not as a force but as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy. It predicts black holes, gravitational waves, and the expansion of the universe — all confirmed observationally. GR underpins the [Standard Model](../theories/standard-model.md) of cosmology, the [no-hair theorem](../concepts/no-hair-theorem.md), and all modern [gravitational wave](../experiments/gravitational-wave-detectors.md) science. Its incompatibility with quantum mechanics at extreme scales (Planck energy, black hole singularities) drives the search for quantum gravity via [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md), [quadratic gravity](../theories/quadratic-gravity.md), and other approaches.
