# KM3NET Monster Neutrino Detection

In approximately 2023, an extraordinarily energetic [neutrino](../concepts/neutrino.md) struck the Mediterranean Sea and activated the partially complete Cubic Kilometer Neutrino Telescope (KM3NET) detector off Sicily's coast. The particle was 35 times more energetic than any previously detected neutrino, including those from the IceCube observatory in Antarctica.

## Conventional Explanations

Scientists initially attributed the neutrino to:
- **Blazars:** Highly active galaxies with jets pointed toward Earth
- **Cosmogenic sources:** Background neutrinos produced by ultra-high-energy cosmic rays interacting with the cosmic microwave background

## The Primordial Black Hole Hypothesis

[David Kaiser](../people/david-kaiser.md) (MIT) proposed an alternative: the neutrino originated from an exploding [primordial black hole](../concepts/primordial-black-holes.md). Kaiser and doctoral student Alexandra Klipfel published their analysis in *Physical Review Letters* (September 18, 2025).

Their calculations showed:
- A PBH with initial asteroid mass, exploding ~2,000 astronomical units away (2,000x Earth-Sun distance), could have produced the neutrino
- **Probability:** ~8% chance — "a low-probability event, but not a completely impossible event" (Klipfel)
- The PBH would have emitted [Hawking radiation](../concepts/hawking-radiation.md) throughout its 13.8-billion-year life before the final explosive burst
- Distance constraints: closer would have produced detectable gamma rays; farther would have dispersed particles too broadly

Kaiser: "The numbers are outrageously congruent, considering I walked in half-joking."

## Reception

- **Skeptical:** Dan Hooper (Wisconsin-Madison): "I would bet an awful lot of money that it has nothing to do with primordial black holes"
- **Cautious:** Evan McDonough (Winnipeg) suggests "there is probably at least one PBH out there" but questions whether they are significant dark matter
- **Hopeful:** Bernard Carr (who developed PBH theory with Hawking): "This could be evidence for black hole explosions"

## Significance

If confirmed, this would be the first observational evidence for both primordial black holes and [Hawking radiation](../concepts/hawking-radiation.md) — two of the most important unconfirmed predictions in theoretical physics. It would also open a new window onto the earliest moments of the universe and the nature of dark matter.

## Related Detectors

The global [neutrino](../concepts/neutrino.md) detector network includes KM3NET, IceCube (Antarctica), Super-Kamiokande (Japan), and the [DUNE Experiment](../experiments/dune-experiment.md) (South Dakota). Each uses different detection media and techniques, giving complementary sensitivity to different neutrino flavors and energies.
