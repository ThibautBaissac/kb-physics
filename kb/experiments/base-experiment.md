# BASE Experiment

BASE (Baryon Antibaryon Symmetry Experiment) is a precision antimatter physics experiment at CERN's Antiproton Decelerator. Its primary goal is to compare the magnetic moments of protons and antiprotons at the highest possible precision, testing [CPT symmetry](../concepts/cpt-symmetry.md).

## How It Works

BASE uses electromagnetic **Penning traps** — devices that confine charged particles using a combination of static magnetic and electric fields — to capture individual antiprotons from CERN's antimatter factory. The trapped particles are held and interrogated for extended periods, allowing extremely precise measurements of their magnetic moments.

Antiprotons behave as quantum-mechanical magnetic dipoles with two possible spin orientations. BASE measures the frequency of transitions between these spin states to determine the magnetic moment.

## Key Results

### Magnetic Moment Comparison

Previous measurements by BASE demonstrated that the magnetic moments of protons and antiprotons are **identical to within parts-per-billion** — the most precise CPT test for baryonic matter to date. These results relied on *incoherent* spectroscopy, limited by magnetic field fluctuations and disruption from the measurement process itself.

### First Antimatter Qubit (2025)

In a landmark result published in *Nature*, BASE achieved the first demonstration of an **antimatter quantum bit**. The team maintained a single antiproton in a coherent quantum superposition of spin states for **50 seconds** — far longer than any previously achieved for individual nuclear magnetic moments in this context.

This was enabled by **coherent quantum transition spectroscopy**, which eliminates the noise sources that plagued earlier incoherent measurements. Stefan Ulmer, BASE spokesperson, described it as: *"The first antimatter qubit [that] opens up the prospect of applying coherent spectroscopy methods to single matter and antimatter systems."*

The 50-second coherence time means [decoherence](../concepts/decoherence.md) — normally catastrophic for quantum systems — was kept at bay in an antimatter system for a record duration.

## Future: BASE-STEP

The next phase, **BASE-STEP**, will transport antiprotons to magnetically quieter environments, potentially extending coherence times by a factor of ten. This could enable magnetic moment measurements 10–100× more precise than current limits, yielding the most stringent CPT test ever performed for [antimatter](../concepts/antimatter.md).

## Connection to BSM Physics

Any discrepancy between proton and antiproton magnetic moments would directly indicate CPT violation — and therefore physics [beyond the Standard Model](../open-questions/beyond-standard-model.md). BASE's coherent spectroscopy technique opens a new experimental frontier for this search.
