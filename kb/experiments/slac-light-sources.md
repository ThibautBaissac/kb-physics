# SLAC Light Sources

SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory operates two major [photon](../concepts/photon.md)-based research facilities at Stanford: the Stanford Synchrotron Radiation Lightsource (SSRL) and the Linac Coherent Light Source (LCLS). Together they exemplify how mastery of photons — in this case, intense X-ray beams — enables science far beyond particle physics.

## Stanford Synchrotron Radiation Lightsource (SSRL)

SSRL has been in operation for over thirty-five years. It accelerates electrons in a storage ring; as they curve under magnetic fields, they emit synchrotron radiation — intense X-ray beams tunable across a wide energy range. Researchers use SSRL to:

- Image cellular structures at molecular resolution
- Study the crystal structures of proteins and catalysts
- Probe solar cell materials at the nanoscale

[Uwe Bergmann](../people/uwe-bergmann.md) (University of Wisconsin-Madison) has used SSRL for landmark humanities research: by detecting the iron signature of ancient ink through X-rays, he recovered Archimedes' mathematical writings from a twelfth-century parchment palimpsest — a manuscript that had been scraped and reused as a prayer book.

## Linac Coherent Light Source (LCLS)

LCLS is a free-electron laser — the world's first hard X-ray free-electron laser when it opened in 2009 — that produces X-ray pulses billions of times brighter than synchrotron sources. These ultrashort pulses (femtoseconds) allow researchers to capture snapshots of individual atomic motion during chemical reactions, opening the field of "molecular movies."

[Claudio Pellegrini](../people/claudio-pellegrini.md) (UCLA), a pioneer of free-electron laser science, notes that recent collaborations at LCLS exploit photon entanglement and quantum properties of light — pushing X-ray science into the quantum regime.

## Significance

SLAC's light sources demonstrate the breadth of photon science: the same particle that underpins the [Standard Model](../theories/standard-model.md) as the electromagnetic force carrier also illuminates ancient manuscripts and films chemical bond-breaking in real time.

## Rubin Operations Center

Beyond its X-ray facilities, SLAC also serves as operating partner for the LSST Camera at the [Vera C. Rubin Observatory](vera-c-rubin-observatory.md) in Chile. A dedicated Rubin Operations Center at the Menlo Park campus provides remote observing support and hosts postdoc training for the Legacy Survey of Space and Time.
