# Dark Energy

<!-- stub: needs enrichment via kb:ingest -->

Dark energy is the unknown component driving the accelerating expansion of the universe, comprising roughly 68% of its total energy density. The simplest explanation is Einstein's [cosmological constant](../concepts/cosmological-constant.md) (a fixed energy density of empty space), but DESI observations (2024) suggest dark energy may be weakening over time — favoring dynamical models. The enormous mismatch between predicted [vacuum energy](../concepts/zero-point-energy.md) and observed dark energy (a factor of ~10^120) is the vacuum energy catastrophe. In 2025, [Bruno Bento](../people/bruno-bento.md) and [Miguel Montero](../people/miguel-montero.md) derived the first explicit [de Sitter](../concepts/de-sitter-space.md) solution from [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md) via [compactification](../concepts/compactification.md), producing dynamical dark energy consistent with DESI data.
