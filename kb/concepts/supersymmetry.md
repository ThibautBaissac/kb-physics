# Supersymmetry

Supersymmetry (SUSY) is a proposed symmetry of nature that relates bosons (force-carrying particles) to fermions (matter particles). Each particle would have a "superpartner" with different spin statistics.

## Motivation

SUSY rose to prominence in the 1970s–80s partly because it offered elegant solutions to major open problems:

- **[Hierarchy problem](../concepts/hierarchy-problem.md):** Superpartner loops would cancel the quantum corrections that otherwise drive the Higgs mass to the Planck scale
- **[Dark matter](../concepts/dark-matter.md):** The lightest stable superpartner (the neutralino) would be a natural WIMP candidate
- **Force unification:** SUSY modifies the running of coupling constants so the three forces unify at high energies

## The LHC Null Result

When the [Large Hadron Collider](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md) began operations in 2010, there was broad consensus that SUSY particles would be discovered soon after the Higgs boson — and the Higgs was found in 2012 at 125 GeV. But SUSY partners did not appear. After 13+ years of operation, no superpartners have been observed.

[Maurizio Pierini](../people/maurizio-pierini.md) (CERN) described the experience: "it's like we set out to win a 100-meter race only to find we were running a marathon." Pierini also characterizes the Higgs mass as "the smoking gun of new physics" — it sits precisely where the Standard Model should break down — yet searches in the surrounding energy range find only "smoke."

[Flip Tanedo](../people/flip-tanedo.md) draws a historical parallel: just as "it was obvious to everybody that they would detect the ether" before Michelson-Morley (1887), physicists entering the LHC era were broadly confident SUSY would appear. The null result, like the ether null result, may ultimately force a deeper reconception.

## Continued Searches

Rather than abandoning SUSY entirely, physicists have expanded to less-explored signatures:

- **Long-lived particles:** Some SUSY scenarios predict superpartners that travel significant distances before decaying, leaving tracks in outer detector regions — signatures not prioritized in early LHC searches
- **Lower-energy data:** The [LHC](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md)'s vast unexplored low-energy dataset may harbor lighter particles with unusual signatures
- **High-Luminosity LHC:** The HL-LHC upgrade will increase the dataset tenfold, improving sensitivity to rare or subtle signatures

## N=4 Maximal Supersymmetry

N=4 supersymmetry is the highest level of supersymmetry possible in four-dimensional [quantum field theory](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md). It is a highly constrained and mathematically elegant framework.

[Henriette Elvang](../people/henriette-elvang.md) demonstrated in her January 2026 paper "String Theory From Maximal Supersymmetry" that [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md) is the unique [UV completion](../concepts/uv-completion.md) for theories with N=4 supersymmetry. This result, derived via the [bootstrap method](../concepts/bootstrap-method.md), shows that string theory emerges necessarily from maximal supersymmetry assumptions without presupposing anything about the higher-energy theory.

[Pedro Vieira](../people/pedro-vieira.md) cautioned that Elvang's derivation currently applies only to tree-level [scattering amplitudes](../concepts/scattering-amplitudes.md). Whether the result extends to the full quantum theory remains an open question.
