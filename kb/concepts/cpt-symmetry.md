# CPT Symmetry

CPT symmetry is a fundamental theorem of quantum field theory stating that all physical processes are invariant under the combined operations of:

- **C (Charge conjugation):** Replace every particle with its antiparticle
- **P (Parity inversion):** Mirror-flip all spatial coordinates
- **T (Time reversal):** Reverse the direction of time

The CPT theorem, proven rigorously in the framework of Lorentz-invariant quantum field theory, is one of the best-tested symmetries in all of physics. Any violation would require new physics that overturns the foundations of the [Standard Model](../theories/standard-model.md).

## What CPT Predicts

CPT invariance requires that particles and their antiparticles have:
- **Identical masses**
- **Identical lifetimes**
- **Identical magnetic moments** (in magnitude)
- **Opposite charges** and quantum numbers

Any measured difference between a particle and its antiparticle in these quantities would directly signal CPT violation — and thus new physics [beyond the Standard Model](../open-questions/beyond-standard-model.md).

## Testing CPT with Antimatter

The most stringent tests of CPT symmetry come from comparing proton and antiproton properties. The [BASE experiment](../experiments/base-experiment.md) at CERN has compared the magnetic moments of protons and antiprotons to parts-per-billion precision, finding no deviation.

In 2025, BASE created the first **antimatter qubit** — an antiproton held in coherent superposition for 50 seconds — enabling coherent quantum transition spectroscopy. This technique eliminates noise sources that limited prior incoherent measurements, promising a 10–100× improvement in precision and thus a 10–100× tighter test of CPT symmetry for baryonic antimatter.

## The Asymmetry Puzzle

Despite CPT symmetry being so well-tested, the universe contains far more [matter than antimatter](../concepts/antimatter.md). This asymmetry is not explained by the Standard Model. Either CPT is subtly violated at levels beyond current precision, or some other mechanism generated the asymmetry in the early universe. Precision antimatter spectroscopy is one of the few experimental paths to resolving this.
