# De Sitter Space

De Sitter space is a maximally symmetric spacetime with positive curvature, arising as a solution to Einstein's general relativity equations when the [cosmological constant](../concepts/cosmological-constant.md) is positive. It describes an empty universe undergoing exponential expansion. Named after Dutch physicist Willem de Sitter, who in 1916-17 published three papers exploring the possible geometries allowed by general relativity.

## Geometry and Properties

De Sitter found that an empty universe with a cosmological constant can take one of three shapes depending on the sign of that constant:

- **Positive** cosmological constant: positively curved de Sitter space (expanding)
- **Negative** cosmological constant: negatively curved [anti-de Sitter space](../concepts/anti-de-sitter-space.md) (contracting)
- **Zero** cosmological constant: flat Minkowski space (static)

In de Sitter space, two otherwise stationary objects move apart over time — like dots on an inflating balloon. Space expands at an exponential rate, creating a [cosmological horizon](../concepts/cosmological-horizon.md) beyond which communication is impossible. As Monica Pate (NYU) describes: "It's expanding so fast that there are parts of space-time which, if you wait forever, you will never be able to see."

## Relevance to Our Universe

Our universe is not exactly de Sitter — it contains both matter and dark energy — but it resembles de Sitter space in two key epochs:

1. **Early universe:** During [cosmic inflation](../concepts/cosmic-inflation.md), the universe underwent rapid exponential expansion closely approximating de Sitter geometry.
2. **Far future:** As expansion continues and matter becomes sparser, the universe is becoming increasingly de Sitter-like. [Daniel Green](../people/daniel-green.md) (UCSD) notes: "People think eventually we will live in pure de Sitter space, or something like it, for a very long time."

## The Quantum Problem

De Sitter space poses severe difficulties for [quantum field theory](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md). Unlike flat or [anti-de Sitter](../concepts/anti-de-sitter-space.md) space, there is no accessible boundary where quantum fluctuations vanish. Green explains: "Gravity is fluctuating, quantum mechanically, everywhere. And there's no place to shield yourself from that."

Key problems include:
- **No observer-system separation:** The machinery of quantum mechanics assumes an external observer measuring a quantum system. In de Sitter space, the observer is always inside the experiment.
- **Energy non-conservation:** The expansion pumps energy into the universe, as [João Penedones](../people/joao-penedones.md) (EPFL) puts it: "The expansion is literally pumping energy, changing the universe."
- **Unstable particles:** Even the concept of a particle breaks down — [Manuel Loparco](../people/manuel-loparco.md) notes "In de Sitter, there is no such thing" as a localized, stable particle.
- **Massive photons:** Penedones and Loparco showed (2025) that massless photons can be composed of massive particles in de Sitter space, with implications for spontaneous photon decay.

See [Quantum Gravity in de Sitter Space](../open-questions/quantum-gravity-in-de-sitter-space.md) for the full scope of this open problem.

## Black Hole Analogy

Physicists have noted a deep similarity between de Sitter space and black holes: both feature horizons that trap information. [Tom Hartman](../people/tom-hartman.md) (Stanford) treats black hole physics as "a warm-up problem for understanding quantum effects and cosmology." However, a key difference remains — a black hole has one horizon, whereas de Sitter space has many observer-dependent horizons, complicating the application of [holography](../concepts/holography.md).

## String Theory Breakthrough (2025)

[String theory](../theories/string-theory.md) long struggled to describe de Sitter space — its best-understood models produced only [anti-de Sitter](../concepts/anti-de-sitter-space.md) or flat geometries. In 2025, [Bruno Bento](../people/bruno-bento.md) and [Miguel Montero](../people/miguel-montero.md) (Madrid) derived the first explicit de Sitter solution from string theory, using a Casimir-like mechanism in [compactified](../concepts/compactification.md) extra dimensions. Thomas Van Riet (KU Leuven): "It is the very first example [from string theory] of an explicit de Sitter space."

The solution produces dynamical (weakening) dark energy, matching DESI observations that the [cosmological constant](../concepts/cosmological-constant.md) may not be constant. Caveats: 5D rather than 4D, and the dark energy value (10^-15 Planck units) is far from the observed 10^-120.
