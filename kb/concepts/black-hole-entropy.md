# Black Hole Entropy

Black hole entropy is the thermodynamic entropy associated with a black hole, proportional to its event horizon area. The Bekenstein-Hawking formula, derived from semiclassical arguments by Jacob Bekenstein and Stephen Hawking, assigns a precise entropy to black holes but leaves open the question of what microscopic degrees of freedom account for it.

## String Theory Derivation (1996)

In 1996, Andrew Strominger and Cumrun Vafa provided the first microscopic derivation of black hole entropy using [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md). By counting the microstates of a specific class of black holes constructed from strings and branes, they exactly recovered the Bekenstein-Hawking formula.

This result is widely regarded as one of string theory's most significant achievements, providing concrete evidence that the theory captures real physics of quantum gravity — even among physicists otherwise skeptical of string theory's broader claims.

## Holography and De Sitter Space

The Bekenstein-Hawking formula — entropy proportional to horizon area — was a key inspiration for the [holographic principle](../concepts/holography.md), which posits that the information in a volume of space is encoded on its boundary. This principle has been successfully applied to black holes and [anti-de Sitter space](../concepts/anti-de-sitter-space.md) via the AdS/CFT correspondence.

Physicists now use black hole holography as a "warm-up" for understanding [de Sitter space](../concepts/de-sitter-space.md), which shares the horizon structure of black holes but with observer-dependent [cosmological horizons](../concepts/cosmological-horizon.md) rather than a single event horizon.

## Hawking Radiation

The Bekenstein-Hawking formula implies black holes have a temperature inversely proportional to their mass. [Hawking radiation](../concepts/hawking-radiation.md) is the thermal emission at this temperature — the mechanism by which black holes slowly evaporate. For [primordial black holes](../concepts/primordial-black-holes.md) of nuclear mass, this evaporation culminates in a dramatic final explosion that may have produced the [KM3NET monster neutrino](../experiments/km3net-neutrino-detection.md).
