# Ultrasoftness

Ultrasoftness is a mathematical property of [scattering amplitudes](../concepts/scattering-amplitudes.md) that prevents infinitesimal concentrations of energy. It describes a specific high-energy behavior where interactions become extremely "soft" — spreading out rather than concentrating.

## Deriving String Theory

In the August 2025 paper "Strings From Almost Nothing," [Cliff Cheung](../people/cliff-cheung.md) and collaborators used the [bootstrap method](../concepts/bootstrap-method.md) to show that assuming ultrasoftness as a physical requirement uniquely yields [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md)'s scattering amplitudes. No other consistent theory satisfies this condition.

## Controversy

Critics, notably Peter Woit, dismissed this result as circular reasoning: ultrasoftness was already known to be a characteristic property of string theory. From this perspective, the derivation assumes a property specific to string theory and then unsurprisingly recovers string theory.

Defenders counter that the significance lies in showing ultrasoftness *alone* — without assuming strings, extra dimensions, or other string-theoretic machinery — is sufficient to derive the full theory. The question shifts to whether ultrasoftness is a physically reasonable assumption on its own merits.
