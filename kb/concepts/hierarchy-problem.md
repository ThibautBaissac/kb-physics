# Hierarchy Problem

The hierarchy problem is the inexplicably enormous disparity between two fundamental scales in physics: the mass of the Higgs boson (~125 GeV, setting the physical scale of atoms) and the Planck scale (~10^19 GeV, associated with quantum gravity). This chasm — atoms are vastly larger than the Planck scale — seems unstable and unnatural in quantum field theory, where quantum corrections should push the Higgs mass up toward the Planck scale.

## Supersymmetric Solution (1981)

Edward Witten proposed that [supersymmetry](../concepts/supersymmetry.md) could solve the hierarchy problem: the existence of additional elementary particles only slightly heavier than the Higgs boson would provide a balancing mechanism, stabilizing the Higgs mass at its observed value. The [LHC](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md)'s collisions should have been energetic enough to produce these supersymmetric partner particles.

## The LHC Null Result

When the LHC ran, only the 25 particles of the [Standard Model](../theories/standard-model.md) were observed. No supersymmetric partners, no other new particles. This null result invalidated the standard reasoning about the hierarchy problem and triggered a crisis in particle physics.

[Cari Cesarotti](../people/cari-cesarotti.md) (CERN) argues: "What we need is humans to read textbooks and sit down and think of new solutions to the hierarchy problem." On the theory side, some hope that a solution could "drop naturally out of the geometry behind [scattering amplitudes](../concepts/scattering-amplitudes.md)."

## Quadratic Gravity Approach (2014)

Alberto Salvio and Alessandro Strumia proposed that [quadratic gravity](../theories/quadratic-gravity.md)'s two extra particles (a scalar and a [ghost](../concepts/ghost-particles.md)) could help push apart the two fundamental scales, offering a potential mechanism for the hierarchy. This connection helped revive interest in the long-dormant theory.

## Status

The hierarchy problem remains unsolved. It is one of the strongest motivations for [physics beyond the Standard Model](../open-questions/beyond-standard-model.md), but the absence of experimental guidance has left theorists without a clear path forward.
