# Ghost Particles

Ghost particles are negative-energy excitations that arise in certain [quantum field theories](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md). Physicists call theories containing ghosts "sick" because they appear to produce mathematical inconsistencies: negative probabilities and runaway vacuum instability.

## The Problem

A ghost carries a minus sign in its energy. This creates two pathological effects:

1. **Vacuum instability:** Spacetime gains energy by creating ghosts, meaning more and more will spontaneously appear, whipping space into an increasingly energetic inferno
2. **Negative probabilities:** Events involving ghosts can have negative probability of occurring — a meaningless proposition

## Ghosts in Quadratic Gravity

[Quadratic gravity](../theories/quadratic-gravity.md) — the unique [renormalizable](../concepts/renormalization.md) quantum field theory of gravity — necessarily contains a ghost particle alongside the normal graviton and a scalar particle. This ghost was the primary reason physicists abandoned the theory after [Kellogg Stelle](../people/kellogg-stelle.md)'s 1977 discovery.

## Modern Ghost-Busting

A growing group of physicists now argues that ghosts may not be fatal after all:

- **Damiano Anselmi** (Pisa) showed that alternative versions of Feynman's rules for describing quantum events can avoid the pitfalls of ghost theories entirely
- **[John Donoghue](../people/john-donoghue.md)** and Gabriel Menezes demonstrated that ghost particles are so unstable they vanish before they can destabilize the vacuum or produce negative probabilities — unitarity (probabilities summing to 100%) is preserved
- **Alberto Salvio** and **Bob Holdom** independently found that tweaking the final step in probability calculations can guarantee positive values
- Donoghue proposed that the real cost of ghosts is temporary **micro-causality violation** — ghost particles can briefly skip backward in time, with our macroscopic sense of time's forward flow emerging as a statistical average

[Luca Buoninfante](../people/luca-buoninfante.md): "Mathematically, they make sense now." Ghosts may be "new objects that appear when we try to understand gravity and quantum field theory at a deeper level."

## Open Status

The question remains open. Alessia Platania (Copenhagen) cautions: "They're working hard. But I would say it's still open as a question." Much work remains to determine whether the various proposed fixes are mutually compatible and whether they hold up in more demanding scenarios.
