# QBism

QBism (Quantum Bayesianism) is an interpretation of quantum mechanics developed primarily by [Chris Fuchs](../people/chris-fuchs.md). It treats quantum mechanics as "a user's manual for the world" — a framework that helps individual agents navigate an unruly and irreducibly surprising reality — rather than as an objective description of physical states.

## Core Claim

In QBism, the [wave function](../concepts/wave-function.md) ψ is not a feature of the world; it is a feature of the agent. Specifically, it is "a catalog of my degrees of belief about the consequences of my actions." Assigning a quantum state to a system is not reporting an objective fact but expressing subjective probability judgments about what outcomes will result from interventions.

Measurement "collapse" is reinterpreted as a Bayesian update: when an agent performs a measurement and obtains an outcome, they revise their beliefs accordingly. This is no different in kind from updating any other probability assignment in light of new evidence. There is no physical collapse, no branching of worlds, no special role for macroscopic observers — just an agent learning from experience.

## Dissolving the Measurement Problem

QBism sidesteps the [measurement problem](../open-questions/measurement-problem.md) by rejecting the premise that the wave function describes a shared, observer-independent reality. Since quantum states are always relative to a particular agent's knowledge and choices, there is nothing to collapse — the "collapse" is simply the agent updating their beliefs.

Similarly, the question "what does ψ describe before measurement?" dissolves: ψ doesn't describe the world at all; it describes what an agent expects.

## Implications

- **First-person perspective:** QBism is irreducibly first-person. No God's-eye view of the universe exists; physics is always the practice of an agent embedded in the world.
- **No shared reality:** Different agents can assign different quantum states to the same system without contradiction, because states are personal, not objective.
- **Anti-reductionist:** The agent cannot be reduced away or derived from the physics; the agent is the starting point.

## Relationship to Other Interpretations

QBism shares with the [Copenhagen interpretation](../open-questions/measurement-problem.md) a focus on knowledge rather than reality, but goes further: where Copenhagen stops at saying ψ describes knowledge, QBism specifies whose knowledge (the individual agent's) and what kind (subjective Bayesian degrees of belief). Unlike [relational quantum mechanics](../concepts/relational-quantum-mechanics.md), QBism does not relativize facts to physical systems interacting with each other — only to agents capable of experiencing outcomes.
