# Anti-de Sitter Space

Anti-de Sitter (AdS) space is a maximally symmetric spacetime with negative curvature, arising from a negative [cosmological constant](../concepts/cosmological-constant.md). Unlike our expanding universe, anti-de Sitter space acts like a box: everything is drawn back toward the center.

## Properties

The edge of an AdS universe is not a physical wall but a conformal boundary that only light can reach. Monica Pate (NYU) describes it as "a constant gravity everywhere" — not uniform, but persistent, always pulling things back. If you broadcast a message or throw a rock in anti-de Sitter space, it will eventually boomerang back to you.

This bounded nature makes AdS space far more tractable for quantum physics than [de Sitter space](../concepts/de-sitter-space.md). Quantum fluctuations go to zero on the boundary, allowing physicists to make precise quantum measurements by setting up experiments on the universe's edge.

## Role in Theoretical Physics

Anti-de Sitter space is the setting for the AdS/CFT correspondence, the most developed realization of [holography](../concepts/holography.md). This duality relates gravity in the bulk of AdS space to a quantum field theory living on its boundary, providing powerful computational tools for [quantum gravity](../open-questions/quantum-gravity-in-de-sitter-space.md) and strongly coupled systems.

While AdS space does not describe our universe (which has a positive cosmological constant), it has been the primary testbed for ideas about quantum gravity precisely because its boundary makes calculations well-defined.

## Contrast with De Sitter Space

| Property | Anti-de Sitter | De Sitter |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| Cosmological constant | Negative | Positive |
| Curvature | Negative | Positive |
| Behavior | Contracting / bounded | Expanding |
| Boundary | Accessible conformal boundary | No accessible boundary |
| Quantum calculations | Well-defined at boundary | Fundamentally problematic |
