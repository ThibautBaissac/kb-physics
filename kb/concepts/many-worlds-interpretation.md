# Many-Worlds Interpretation

<!-- stub: needs enrichment via kb:ingest -->

The many-worlds interpretation (MWI), proposed by Hugh Everett III in 1957, holds that the [wave function](../concepts/wave-function.md) never collapses. Instead, every quantum measurement causes the universe to branch into multiple copies, each realizing a different outcome. There is no special role for observers or measurement — the universe evolves unitarily at all times. MWI is one of several competing interpretations of quantum mechanics alongside [QBism](../concepts/qbism.md), [Relational QM](../concepts/relational-quantum-mechanics.md), and the [Copenhagen interpretation](../concepts/copenhagen-interpretation.md). [Wojciech Zurek](../people/wojciech-zurek.md)'s [decoherence](../concepts/decoherence.md) and [quantum Darwinism](../concepts/quantum-darwinism.md) program aims to reconcile Copenhagen and many-worlds by deriving the appearance of collapse from standard quantum mechanics.
