# Hawking Radiation

Hawking radiation is the theoretical process by which black holes emit particles through quantum effects at their event horizons, gradually losing mass and eventually evaporating completely. Predicted by Stephen Hawking in 1974, it connects black hole physics to quantum mechanics and thermodynamics.

## The Evaporation Process

Black holes radiate particles at a rate inversely proportional to their mass — smaller black holes radiate faster. For a [primordial black hole](../concepts/primordial-black-holes.md) originally the size of an atomic nucleus:

- The vast majority of its lifetime is spent radiating very slowly
- Alexandra Klipfel (MIT): "Very little mass gets radiated over the majority of the black hole's lifetime. But then, right at the end, it emits a majority of its mass in a very rapid explosion."
- The final burst releases all 17 fundamental particles of the Standard Model

## Connection to Black Hole Thermodynamics

Hawking radiation is intimately connected to [black hole entropy](../concepts/black-hole-entropy.md). The Bekenstein-Hawking formula — entropy proportional to horizon area — implies that black holes are thermal objects with a temperature inversely proportional to their mass. Hawking radiation is the thermal emission corresponding to this temperature.

## Observational Status

Hawking radiation has never been directly observed from astrophysical black holes (stellar-mass black holes radiate far too slowly). The most promising route to detection is through [primordial black holes](../concepts/primordial-black-holes.md) that are completing their evaporation today, producing detectable bursts of particles including the [KM3NET monster neutrino](../experiments/km3net-neutrino-detection.md).

Dan Hooper (Wisconsin-Madison) is skeptical: "I would bet an awful lot of money that it has nothing to do with primordial black holes," arguing that exploding black holes would be easily detectable if they existed. Bernard Carr, who developed the theory with Hawking 50 years ago, is more hopeful: "This could be evidence for black hole explosions."

## Information Paradox Connection

Hawking radiation sits at the center of the [black hole information paradox](../open-questions/black-hole-information-paradox.md). If the outgoing radiation is truly thermal and featureless (as Hawking's original calculation suggests), then when a black hole fully evaporates, all information about what fell in is destroyed — violating quantum mechanics. The [no-hair theorem](../concepts/no-hair-theorem.md) compounds this: if black holes carry no distinguishing features, there is no obvious mechanism by which information could escape.

Proposed resolutions — firewalls, fuzzballs, holography — all modify either the nature of the horizon or the character of Hawking radiation to allow information to escape. Gravitational wave tests of the no-hair conjecture (constraining horizon-scale structure to within 40 km as of 2025) provide empirical guidance on which alternatives remain viable.
