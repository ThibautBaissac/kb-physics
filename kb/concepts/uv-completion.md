# UV Completion

A UV (ultraviolet) completion is a theory that remains well-defined at arbitrarily high energies, resolving the infinities or inconsistencies that arise in a lower-energy effective theory. The term "ultraviolet" refers to the high-energy/short-distance regime by analogy with the electromagnetic spectrum.

## String Theory as UV Completion

[String theory](../theories/string-theory.md) is widely considered a candidate UV completion of gravity and [quantum field theory](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md). The extended nature of strings smooths out the point-particle singularities that produce infinities in quantum gravity calculations.

[Henriette Elvang](../people/henriette-elvang.md) demonstrated that string theory is the *unique* UV completion for [quantum field theories](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md) with N=4 [supersymmetry](../concepts/supersymmetry.md). This result uses the [bootstrap method](../concepts/bootstrap-method.md) to show that no alternative high-energy theory can consistently extend maximal supersymmetry.

## Foundational Questions

[Astrid Eichhorn](../people/astrid-eichhorn.md) raised the question of whether the UV regime might involve spacetime configurations so extremely curved that the flat-space [scattering amplitude](../concepts/scattering-amplitudes.md) framework — on which current bootstrap results rely — becomes meaningless.
