# Toponium

Toponium is a quasi-bound state consisting of a top quark and an anti-top quark briefly held together by gluon exchange. It was predicted theoretically in 1990 but was long considered too subtle an effect to detect at the [Large Hadron Collider](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md). Two independent CMS and ATLAS analyses confirmed its existence at the LHC in 2025.

## Why Toponium Is Hard to Detect

The top quark is the heaviest known elementary particle in the [Standard Model](../theories/standard-model.md), decaying in roughly 10⁻²⁵ seconds — far too quickly to form conventional hadrons like other quarks do. Yet quantum mechanics permits a narrow window: top quark-antiquark pairs produced nearly at rest relative to each other can occasionally exchange gluons and bind into a transient toponium state before decaying. This is only observable when the pair is produced at near-threshold energy.

## Discovery (2025)

### CMS Analysis

CMS researchers analyzed 2016–2018 Run-2 data while originally searching for Higgs boson variants. They found an unexpected surplus of top quark-antiquark pairs at minimum production energy — the signature of a quasi-bound state. Their measurement:

- **Cross section:** 8.8 ± 1.3 picobarns
- **Significance:** 5σ (the particle physics discovery threshold)

The result was presented at the European Physical Society's High-Energy Physics (EPS-HEP) conference in Marseille in July 2025.

### ATLAS Confirmation

ATLAS independently analysed their full Run-2 dataset (2015–2018) and confirmed the effect:

- **Cross section:** 9.0 ± 1.3 picobarns
- **Significance:** 7.7σ (rejecting models that omit quasi-bound-state formation)

The two measurements are in close agreement.

### Quantum Information Route

A parallel discovery route came from [quantum information](../concepts/quantum-entanglement.md) measurements. When CMS and ATLAS developed methods to detect [magic states](../concepts/magic-states.md) in entangled top quark pairs, the extra entanglement signature from toponium formation became visible as an unexpected spin-off. [Marcel Vos](../people/marcel-vos.md), ATLAS's top quark research leader, described this as "our first tangible spin-off from all this."

## Remaining Uncertainty

Although the excess is firmly established, its precise interpretation is still open. Two explanations fit the data:

1. **Toponium:** gluon-mediated binding into the predicted top-antitop bound state
2. **Unknown particle:** a new resonance with mass close to twice the top quark mass

Resolving this ambiguity requires advanced [quantum chromodynamics](../concepts/quantum-chromodynamics.md) calculations modelling quark-gluon behaviour in the non-relativistic regime.

## Historical Context: The Quarkonium Family

Toponium would complete the known quarkonium family:

| State | Quark content | Discovered | Location |
|-------|---------------|------------|----------|
| Charmonium (J/ψ) | charm-anticharm | 1974 ("November Revolution") | SLAC / BNL |
| Bottomonium (Υ) | bottom-antibottom | 1977 | Fermilab |
| Toponium | top-antitop | 2025 | CERN/LHC |

Run 3 of the LHC, collecting substantially more data, will sharpen these measurements and potentially distinguish the two interpretations.
