# Toponium

Toponium is a bound state consisting of a top quark and an anti-top quark. It was predicted theoretically in 1990 but was long considered too subtle an effect to detect at the [Large Hadron Collider](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md).

## Discovery

The detection of toponium came as an unexpected by-product of quantum information measurements at the LHC. When the CMS and ATLAS experiments developed sensitive methods to measure [magic states](../concepts/magic-states.md) in entangled top quark pairs, they discovered that top quarks and anti-top quarks sometimes exhibited extra [entanglement](../concepts/quantum-entanglement.md) — the signature of binding into toponium.

CMS posted toponium measurements in March 2025, followed by ATLAS in July 2025. [Marcel Vos](../people/marcel-vos.md), ATLAS's top quark research leader, described it as "our first tangible spin-off from all this."

## Significance

Toponium is notable because the top quark is the heaviest known elementary particle in the [Standard Model](../theories/standard-model.md). Top quarks decay in roughly 10^-25 seconds — before they can form conventional hadrons — making the brief window in which they can bind into toponium extremely narrow. The fact that quantum information techniques enabled its detection demonstrates how the convergence of particle physics and quantum information theory can reveal previously inaccessible phenomena.
