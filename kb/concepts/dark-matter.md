# Dark Matter

<!-- stub: needs enrichment via kb:ingest -->

Dark matter is an unknown form of matter that interacts gravitationally but not (or barely) electromagnetically. It accounts for roughly 27% of the universe's energy density and is required to explain galaxy rotation curves, gravitational lensing, and cosmic structure formation. Leading candidates include WIMPs (weakly interacting massive particles), axions, and [primordial black holes](../concepts/primordial-black-holes.md). The [Large Hadron Collider](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md) has found no dark matter particles, making it a central part of the [beyond Standard Model](../open-questions/beyond-standard-model.md) puzzle. The [KM3NET monster neutrino](../experiments/km3net-neutrino-detection.md) detection may provide indirect evidence if primordial black holes turn out to constitute some fraction of dark matter.
