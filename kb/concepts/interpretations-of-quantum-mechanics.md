# Interpretations of Quantum Mechanics

<!-- stub: needs enrichment via kb:ingest -->

Quantum mechanics is the most successful physical theory ever developed, yet physicists disagree profoundly on what it means. At the 2025 Helgoland centennial — 100 years after [Heisenberg](../people/werner-heisenberg.md)'s breakthrough — roughly 300 physicists debated competing interpretations with no consensus.

The major interpretations in this KB:

- **[Copenhagen Interpretation](../concepts/copenhagen-interpretation.md)**: The textbook view — [wave function](../concepts/wave-function.md) collapse upon measurement, no deeper explanation.
- **[Many-Worlds Interpretation](../concepts/many-worlds-interpretation.md)**: No collapse — every measurement branches the universe.
- **[QBism](../concepts/qbism.md)**: Wave functions are personal probability assignments by agents, not descriptions of reality ([Chris Fuchs](../people/chris-fuchs.md)).
- **[Relational Quantum Mechanics](../concepts/relational-quantum-mechanics.md)**: Properties exist only relative to interactions between systems ([Carlo Rovelli](../people/carlo-rovelli.md)).
- **[Quantum Darwinism](../concepts/quantum-darwinism.md)**: [Decoherence](../concepts/decoherence.md) and environmental selection explain the classical world without collapse ([Wojciech Zurek](../people/wojciech-zurek.md)).

The unresolved [measurement problem](../open-questions/measurement-problem.md) — why do we see definite outcomes? — remains the central question connecting all interpretations.
