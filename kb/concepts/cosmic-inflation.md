# Cosmic Inflation

Cosmic inflation is a period of rapid, exponential expansion in the very early universe. During inflation, the universe closely resembled [de Sitter space](../concepts/de-sitter-space.md) — a spacetime dominated by a positive [cosmological constant](../concepts/cosmological-constant.md) (or an effective equivalent driven by the inflaton field).

## Connection to De Sitter Space

Our universe likely looked a lot like de Sitter space during inflation. After inflation ended, the universe transitioned to a flatter geometry thanks to the presence of matter and radiation. But as expansion continues and matter becomes sparser, the universe is once again approaching de Sitter geometry.

This means de Sitter space bookends cosmic history: it approximates the geometry of both the very early and the very late universe.
