# Page-Wootters Mechanism

The Page-Wootters mechanism, proposed by Don Page and William Wootters in 1983, describes how time can emerge as an apparent property from quantum entanglement, rather than existing as a fundamental feature of nature.

## The Idea

In this framework, the universe as a whole is in a static, timeless quantum state. However, internal observers perceive temporal evolution through the entangled spatial configurations of subsystems. Time is not built into the fabric of reality — it is an emergent property arising from correlations between entangled parts of the universe.

## Experimental Demonstrations

The mechanism was first demonstrated experimentally with photons in 2013, confirming that entangled quantum systems can exhibit the appearance of temporal evolution even when the global state is static.

## Connection to Particle Physics

[Regina Demina](../people/regina-demina.md) (University of Rochester) has proposed testing the Page-Wootters mechanism using entangled top quark pairs at the [Large Hadron Collider](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md). She envisions "performing this experiment in a system of elementary particles, to demonstrate the Page-Wootters mechanism" — which would extend the demonstration from photons to the most massive known particles.

This connects to broader questions about the [quantum-to-classical transition](../open-questions/measurement-problem.md) and the nature of [entanglement](../concepts/quantum-entanglement.md) in fundamental physics.
