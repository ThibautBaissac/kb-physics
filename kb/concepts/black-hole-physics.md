# Black Hole Physics

<!-- stub: needs enrichment via kb:ingest -->

Black holes sit at the intersection of general relativity, quantum mechanics, and thermodynamics. This overview connects the major black hole topics in the KB.

## Thermodynamics
- **[Black Hole Entropy](../concepts/black-hole-entropy.md)**: Bekenstein and [Hawking](../people/stephen-hawking.md) showed black holes have thermodynamic entropy proportional to their surface area. Strominger and Vafa derived this microscopically from [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md) in 1996.
- **[Hawking Radiation](../concepts/hawking-radiation.md)**: Black holes are not truly black — they emit thermal radiation and gradually evaporate.

## Mergers and Gravitational Waves
- **[Massive Black Hole Binaries](../concepts/massive-black-hole-binaries.md)**: Pairs of supermassive black holes (10⁵–10⁸ M☉) that spiral together and coalesce — the most energetic events since the Big Bang. Detectable in the millihertz band by [LISA](../experiments/lisa.md) and Taiji; serve as standard sirens for cosmology.
- **[No-Hair Theorem](../concepts/no-hair-theorem.md)**: Black holes are characterized by only mass and spin. [Gravitational wave detectors](../experiments/gravitational-wave-detectors.md) constrain any deviation to within 40 km of the horizon.

## Origin and Formation
- **[Primordial Black Holes](../concepts/primordial-black-holes.md)**: Formed from density fluctuations in the early universe — dark matter candidates detectable via Hawking radiation.
- **[Supermassive Black Hole Origins](../open-questions/supermassive-black-hole-origins.md)**: [JWST](../experiments/james-webb-space-telescope.md) found massive black holes existing before galaxies, challenging standard formation models.

## Information and Holography
- **[Black Hole Information Paradox](../open-questions/black-hole-information-paradox.md)**: Does information survive evaporation? The deepest conflict between [GR](../theories/general-relativity.md) and quantum mechanics.
- **[Holography](../concepts/holography.md)** and the **[Island Formula](../concepts/island-formula.md)**: Modern tools for tracking information in black hole interiors.
