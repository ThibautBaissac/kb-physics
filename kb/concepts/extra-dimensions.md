# Extra Dimensions

[String theory](../theories/string-theory.md) requires ten spacetime dimensions: the four familiar dimensions (three spatial plus time) and six additional spatial dimensions. These extra dimensions are theorized to be "compactified" — curled up at extraordinarily small scales, rendering them invisible to current experiments.

## Role in String Theory

The geometry of the compactified dimensions determines the low-energy physics observed in four dimensions. Different compactification geometries produce different particle spectra, coupling constants, and cosmological parameters. The vast number of possible compactifications gives rise to the [string landscape](../concepts/string-landscape.md) of at least 10^500 configurations.

## Observational Status

The extra dimensions exist at scales presumably impossible to detect with foreseeable technology, contributing to the [testability challenges](../open-questions/testability-of-string-theory.md) facing string theory.

## Compactification and Dark Energy

The process of curling up extra dimensions — [compactification](../concepts/compactification.md) — is central to connecting string theory to observable physics. In 2025, Bento and Montero showed that a Casimir-like effect within compactified manifolds can generate positive dark energy, producing the first explicit [de Sitter](../concepts/de-sitter-space.md) solution from string theory. Their approach used Riemann-flat (torus-like) manifolds and started from M-theory (11D, 7 extra dimensions) rather than standard string theory (10D, 6 extra).
