# Antimatter

Antimatter is the mirror-image counterpart to ordinary matter. For every particle, there exists a corresponding antiparticle with the same mass but opposite electric charge and quantum numbers. The antiproton, for instance, has the same mass as a proton but carries a negative charge; the positron matches the electron in mass but carries positive charge.

When matter and antimatter meet, they annihilate, converting entirely to energy — most famously as photon pairs.

## The Matter-Antimatter Asymmetry

The [Standard Model](../theories/standard-model.md) requires that the Big Bang should have produced matter and antimatter in equal quantities. Yet the observable universe is composed almost entirely of matter. This asymmetry — one of the deepest unsolved puzzles in physics — is a primary motivation for precision antimatter experiments and a key driver of the search for [physics beyond the Standard Model](../open-questions/beyond-standard-model.md).

Sakharov's conditions (1967) identify three requirements for generating a matter excess: baryon number violation, [CPT symmetry](../concepts/cpt-symmetry.md) violation, and departure from thermal equilibrium. The Standard Model satisfies none of these at sufficient levels, implying unknown new physics must be responsible.

## Precision Measurements

The most precise tests of matter–antimatter symmetry compare the properties of particles and their antiparticles. The [BASE experiment](../experiments/base-experiment.md) at CERN has measured the magnetic moments of protons and antiprotons to parts-per-billion precision, finding them identical — consistent with [CPT symmetry](../concepts/cpt-symmetry.md).

In 2025, BASE achieved a landmark: trapping an antiproton in a coherent quantum superposition for 50 seconds, creating the first **antimatter qubit**. This opens the door to coherent spectroscopy methods that could yield 10–100× more precise measurements of antiproton magnetic moments.

## Antihydrogen

The ALPHA experiment at CERN creates antihydrogen atoms (antiproton + positron) and studies their spectra. Any difference from ordinary hydrogen spectra would signal CPT violation.
