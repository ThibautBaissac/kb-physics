# Scattering Amplitudes

Scattering amplitudes are the mathematical objects that encode the probabilities of particle interactions. They are the primary observables in both [quantum field theory](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md) and [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md), and have become a central focus of modern theoretical physics.

## Role in the Bootstrap Program

The [bootstrap method](../concepts/bootstrap-method.md) uses scattering amplitudes as the starting point for deriving theories. By imposing physical consistency conditions on amplitudes — symmetry, unitarity, analyticity — researchers constrain what theories are possible.

[Cliff Cheung](../people/cliff-cheung.md) and collaborators showed that assuming [ultrasoftness](../concepts/ultrasoftness.md) (a specific high-energy behavior of amplitudes) uniquely yields string theory's scattering amplitudes. [Henriette Elvang](../people/henriette-elvang.md) derived string theory amplitudes from maximal [supersymmetry](../concepts/supersymmetry.md) assumptions.

## Tree-Level vs. Loop-Level

[Pedro Vieira](../people/pedro-vieira.md) noted an important caveat: current bootstrap derivations of string theory hold only at **tree level** (classical approximation). The full quantum picture, including loop corrections, remains unresolved.

## Foundational Questions

[Astrid Eichhorn](../people/astrid-eichhorn.md) and [Latham Boyle](../people/latham-boyle.md) questioned whether flat-space scattering amplitudes are even the right framework for the universe's highest energies, where spacetime may be too curved for the standard scattering picture to apply.

## Amplitudeology: The Experimental Frontier

"Amplitudeology" — the study of geometric patterns underlying scattering amplitudes — has become one of the most vibrant research programs in theoretical physics. [Adam Falkowski](../people/adam-falkowski.md) describes it as "where the best things have happened," noting the hope that machine learning could accelerate progress.

At the [LHC](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md), AI-assisted analysis now measures amplitudes more precisely than human-made algorithms, improving sensitivity to indirect signs of physics [beyond the Standard Model](../open-questions/beyond-standard-model.md). On the theory side, some hope that the geometry behind amplitudes could yield a natural solution to the [hierarchy problem](../concepts/hierarchy-problem.md).

The field seeks to reformulate particle physics in a different mathematical language that might extend to quantum gravity — bridging the gap between the [Standard Model](../theories/standard-model.md) and a more complete theory.
