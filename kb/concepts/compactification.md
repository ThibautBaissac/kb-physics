# Compactification

Compactification is the process by which the [extra dimensions](../concepts/extra-dimensions.md) of [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md) (or M-theory) shrink down and curl up into a shape so tiny that traveling along one brings you almost instantly back to the starting point. The precise geometry of the "manifold" housing these extra dimensions dictates the properties of all particles and forces observed in nature.

The vast number of possible compactification geometries gives rise to the [string landscape](../concepts/string-landscape.md) of at least 10^500 configurations.

## The Casimir Approach to Dark Energy

In 2025, [Bruno Bento](../people/bruno-bento.md) and [Miguel Montero](../people/miguel-montero.md) (Madrid) found the first explicit [de Sitter](../concepts/de-sitter-space.md) solution from string theory by applying a Casimir-like mechanism to compactification.

### The Casimir Effect in Extra Dimensions

Just as the Casimir effect restricts quantum fluctuations between conducting plates (producing a force that pushes them together), the interior of a compactified manifold restricts fluctuations, generating an analogous force. David Andriot (CNRS): "That's their key ingredient."

Bento and Montero counterbalanced this Casimir force with a **flux** — field lines winding through the extra dimensions that try to expand the manifold's volume. The balance between these opposing forces produces a small, positive dark energy — the hallmark of a [de Sitter universe](../concepts/de-sitter-space.md).

### Choice of Geometry

Bento and Montero chose **Riemann-flat manifolds** (torus-like shapes) for the extra dimensions — "a simple shape" that made calculations tractable. This contrasted with Eva Silverstein's (Stanford) earlier 2021 approach using negatively curved hyperbolic manifolds, which made calculations dramatically harder.

Their result was independently confirmed by Dall'Agata and Zwirner (Padua) using complementary techniques with similar geometry.

### Starting from M-Theory

To simplify further, Bento and Montero started from M-theory (11 dimensions, 7 extra) rather than standard string theory (10 dimensions, 6 extra). M-theory has fewer ingredients despite more dimensions. Subtracting the 6 compactified dimensions from 11 total yielded a 5D de Sitter universe — one dimension too many.

## Caveats

- **5D, not 4D:** The solution describes a 5-dimensional universe; reducing to our 4D world remains a top priority
- **Unstable dark energy:** The dark energy is dynamical and will weaken over time — easier to get from string theory than a fixed [cosmological constant](../concepts/cosmological-constant.md)
- **Wrong magnitude:** The computed value (10^-15 in Planck units) is far from the observed 10^-120, though it is positive and small
