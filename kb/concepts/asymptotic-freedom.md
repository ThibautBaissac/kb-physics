# Asymptotic Freedom

Asymptotic freedom is a property of certain [quantum field theories](../theories/quantum-field-theory.md) where the force becomes weaker as the energy of interactions increases. At arbitrarily high energies, the coupling effectively vanishes, making calculations tractable. The concept was first discovered in quantum chromodynamics (the strong nuclear force) by David Gross, Frank Wilczek, and David Politzer, earning them the 2004 Nobel Prize.

## Asymptotic Freedom in Quadratic Gravity

[John Donoghue](../people/john-donoghue.md) and collaborators discovered that [quadratic gravity](../theories/quadratic-gravity.md) is asymptotically free: as graviton collisions become more intense, gravity gets weaker. Combined with the theory's [renormalizability](../concepts/renormalization.md), this suggests quadratic gravity never breaks down and could describe physics at arbitrarily small scales — potentially qualifying as a complete [UV completion](../concepts/uv-completion.md) of gravity.

Donoghue: "This could allow it to be the final theory." However, he cautioned: "I'm not convinced that this is the final theory." Quadratic gravity may instead function as a self-consistent effective layer of reality, valid to arbitrarily small scales without necessarily being the ultimate description.
