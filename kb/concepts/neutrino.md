# Neutrino

Neutrinos are elementary particles in the [Standard Model](../theories/standard-model.md): electrically neutral, nearly massless fermions that interact only via the weak nuclear force and gravity. Their extreme reluctance to interact with matter — a neutrino can pass through a light-year of lead with only a 50% chance of interaction — makes them both frustratingly difficult to detect and uniquely valuable as astrophysical messengers.

## Properties

- **Three flavors:** electron neutrino (νₑ), muon neutrino (νμ), tau neutrino (ντ), each paired with a corresponding charged lepton
- **Tiny mass:** Non-zero but extraordinarily small (< 0.1 eV); the exact values are unknown — a major gap in the Standard Model
- **Neutral charge:** Unaffected by electromagnetic fields
- **Weak-only interaction:** Penetrate planets, stars, and detector walls with ease
- **Oscillation:** Neutrinos change flavor as they travel — proof of non-zero mass, first confirmed in 1998 (Super-Kamiokande)

## Why Neutrinos Matter

Because they escape environments that are opaque to photons, neutrinos carry direct information from:
- **Stellar cores during collapse** — the inside of a [supernova](../concepts/supernova.md), carrying ~99% of its energy
- **The Sun's nuclear furnace** — solar neutrinos reveal the pp chain in real time
- **The Big Bang** — a relic neutrino background analogous to the CMB should exist, though it has not yet been detected
- **Extreme astrophysical accelerators** — the [KM3NET monster neutrino](../experiments/km3net-neutrino-detection.md), 35× more energetic than any prior detection, may trace to a blazar, cosmogenic interaction, or even an exploding [primordial black hole](../concepts/primordial-black-holes.md)

## Detection Challenges

Standard Model neutrino cross-sections are minuscule, so detectors must be enormous to catch a handful of events. Techniques include:
- **Water/ice Cherenkov:** Neutrinos occasionally scatter electrons or induce charged-current reactions; the resulting relativistic charged particles emit Cherenkov light (IceCube, Super-Kamiokande, KM3NET)
- **Liquid argon TPC:** Electron neutrinos interact with argon nuclei via charged-current reactions, producing visible ionization tracks — the technique used by [DUNE](../experiments/dune-experiment.md)
- **Radiochemical:** Chemical extraction of reaction products from large tanks (early solar neutrino experiments)

## Open Questions

Neutrino physics harbors some of the most pressing open questions in particle physics:
- Are neutrinos their own antiparticles (Majorana fermions)? If so, they could explain matter-antimatter asymmetry via leptogenesis
- What are the absolute neutrino masses?
- Is there a sterile neutrino flavor beyond the three known?
- Do neutrinos have non-standard interactions pointing toward [beyond-Standard-Model physics](../open-questions/beyond-standard-model.md)?
