# Island Formula

The island formula is a quantum gravity tool developed in 2019 by [Juan Maldacena](../people/juan-maldacena.md) and colleagues at the Institute for Advanced Study. It tracks regions ("islands") within black holes and helped explain how black holes can release information despite their gravitational barriers — a key advance in the [black hole information paradox](../concepts/black-hole-entropy.md).

## Origins in Holography

The formula builds on [holography](../concepts/holography.md) and the AdS/CFT correspondence, which Maldacena pioneered in 1997. Using [anti-de Sitter](../concepts/anti-de-sitter-space.md) geometry — a universe with boundaries — string theory calculations can be simplified by treating the interior as shadows projected onto the boundary.

## Application to Closed Universes

When Maldacena applied the island formula to closed universes — spacetimes with no edges or boundaries — the results were disturbing. The formula showed that closed universes possess **one-dimensional [Hilbert spaces](../concepts/hilbert-space.md)**, meaning they contain only one possible quantum state with zero information content.

[Ying Zhao](../people/ying-zhao.md), one of Maldacena's collaborators, recalled being "pretty shocked by that argument" and initially tried to refute it. The result has become a central puzzle in [quantum gravity](../open-questions/quantum-gravity-in-de-sitter-space.md), potentially requiring the introduction of observers to restore the informational complexity of the universe.
