# A Quantum Leap for Antimatter Measurements

The BASE collaboration at CERN has achieved a breakthrough in antimatter research by keeping an antiproton oscillating between two quantum states for nearly a minute while trapped. This represents the first demonstration of an antimatter quantum bit, or qubit.

## The Achievement

Antiprotons, which are antimatter counterparts to protons with identical mass but opposite electrical charge, behave like miniature bar magnets with quantum mechanical spin. Researchers used coherent quantum transition spectroscopy to measure how these magnetic moments flip, enabling high-precision tests of fundamental physics laws.

The research addresses a central mystery in physics: charge-parity-time (CPT) symmetry predicts matter and antimatter should behave identically, yet matter vastly outweighs antimatter in the universe.

## Technical Details

The BASE experiment uses electromagnetic Penning traps to store antiprotons from CERN's antimatter factory. Previous measurements demonstrated the magnetic moments of protons and antiprotons are identical within parts-per-billion. However, those results relied on incoherent spectroscopy disrupted by magnetic field fluctuations.

The new coherent spectroscopy method achieved a spin coherence time of 50 seconds — maintaining quantum control far longer than previously possible for individual antiprotons. This is the first demonstration of coherence in a single nuclear magnetic moment in this context.

## Future Implications

Stefan Ulmer, BASE spokesperson, states: "This represents the first antimatter qubit and opens up the prospect of applying coherent spectroscopy methods to single matter and antimatter systems."

Future improvements through BASE-STEP, which transports antiparticles to magnetically stable environments, could extend coherence times tenfold, enabling "10- to 100-fold improved precision" in future antiproton moment measurements, significantly advancing CPT symmetry tests for baryonic antimatter.

The research was published in *Nature*.
