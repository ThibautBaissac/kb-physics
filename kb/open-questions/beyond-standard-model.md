# Beyond the Standard Model

The search for physics beyond the [Standard Model](../theories/standard-model.md) (BSM) is the central challenge of modern particle physics. The Standard Model's 25 particles and three forces are experimentally confirmed to extraordinary precision, yet fundamental questions remain unanswered.

## Motivations

- **Dark matter:** No Standard Model particle accounts for the ~27% of the universe composed of dark matter
- **Matter-antimatter asymmetry:** The Standard Model cannot explain why matter dominates — [antimatter](../concepts/antimatter.md) should have been produced in equal quantities at the Big Bang
- **The [hierarchy problem](../concepts/hierarchy-problem.md):** The unnatural gap between the Higgs and Planck scales
- **Gravity:** General relativity is not incorporated
- **The Big Bang:** No mechanism for the universe's origin

## The Crisis

The [LHC](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md) was expected to reveal BSM physics — particularly [supersymmetric](../concepts/supersymmetry.md) partners predicted to solve the [hierarchy problem](../concepts/hierarchy-problem.md). Instead, only Standard Model particles were found. This null result, confirmed with increasing precision over 13+ years, has left the field without experimental guidance.

[Maurizio Pierini](../people/maurizio-pierini.md) (CERN) described the experience with a vivid metaphor: "it's like we set out to win a 100-meter race only to find we were running a marathon." He also notes that the [Higgs boson](../concepts/higgs-boson.md) mass of 125 GeV sits precisely where new physics was predicted to appear — "the smoking gun of new physics" — yet searches find only "smoke."

[Flip Tanedo](../people/flip-tanedo.md) frames the situation with a historical parallel: before the 1887 Michelson-Morley experiment, "it was obvious to everybody that they would detect the ether." That null result eventually led to Einstein's special relativity. [Matthew McCullough](../people/matthew-mccullough.md) holds an optimistic view: disproving favored theories "winds up deepening our understanding" — null results, like the Michelson-Morley result, may force a deeper reconception rather than signal the end of progress.

[Adam Falkowski](../people/adam-falkowski.md) predicted in 2012 that "the number of jobs in particle physics will steadily decrease, and particle physicists will die out naturally." The brain drain is real: talented physicists have left for AI and data science. Jared Kaplan (Anthropic co-founder, former amplitude researcher with Nima Arkani-Hamed) left physics in 2019, calling AI "the most important thing to happen while we're alive."

## Where Hope Remains

### Experimental

- **Hidden valleys at the LHC:** [Matt Strassler](../people/matt-strassler.md) (Harvard) argues lighter novel particles could hide in unexplored low-energy data — "There are many opportunities for nature to provide clues at low energies"
- **[Future colliders](../experiments/future-colliders.md):** FCC, muon collider, super-tau-charm facility — but none with discovery guarantees. The [muon collider](../experiments/future-colliders.md) is especially promising: [muons](../concepts/muon.md) are elementary (clean collisions) yet massive (high energies), targeting WIMP dark matter, Higgs vacuum dynamics, and early-universe SM structure
- **[Dark matter](../concepts/dark-matter.md) searches:** Four complementary strategies — indirect detection (energy signatures from interactions), direct detection (nuclear recoils underground), accelerator production (missing momentum at the LHC), and self-interaction studies (galaxy density profiles)
- **Axion searches:** Ultralight dark matter candidates (far lighter than WIMPs) detectable with specialized low-energy experiments rather than high-energy colliders
- **Long-lived particles:** Some [SUSY](../concepts/supersymmetry.md) scenarios predict particles that travel significant distances before decaying, leaving unusual tracks in outer detector regions — signatures not prioritized in early LHC searches
- **[Higgs coupling precision](../concepts/higgs-boson.md):** The Standard Model predicts Higgs couplings scale with particle mass. Confirming this for second-generation quarks (Higgs → charm) and eventually first-generation would either validate the SM or reveal deviations pointing to new physics. Machine-learning jet taggers have recently brought Higgs-to-charm sensitivity within ~4–5× of the SM prediction — closer than anyone expected before the HL-LHC era
- **[Primordial black holes](../concepts/primordial-black-holes.md):** Could constitute dark matter; the [KM3NET monster neutrino](../experiments/km3net-neutrino-detection.md) may be evidence of a PBH evaporation via [Hawking radiation](../concepts/hawking-radiation.md)
- **Precision measurements:** Thorium-229 decay could reveal variations in fundamental constants
- **[CPT symmetry](../concepts/cpt-symmetry.md) tests:** The [BASE experiment](../experiments/base-experiment.md) at CERN compares proton and antiproton magnetic moments to parts-per-billion; the 2025 first antimatter qubit (50-second coherence time) opens a path to 10–100× more precise CPT tests
- **[Muon g-2](../experiments/muon-g2.md):** The Fermilab Muon g-2 experiment's final 2025 result (127 ppb precision) finds the muon's anomalous magnetic moment aμ = 0.001 165 920 705. For years this hovered ~3–4σ above Standard Model predictions, hinting at new physics. New lattice QCD calculations (BMW) have narrowed this gap — the theoretical interpretation remains actively debated. If the discrepancy is confirmed by improved lattice calculations, it would be the strongest direct hint of BSM physics yet

### Theoretical

- **[Scattering amplitudes](../concepts/scattering-amplitudes.md) / "amplitudeology":** A vibrant program reformulating particle physics in geometric language that could extend to quantum gravity. Falkowski: "That's where the best things have happened"
- **[Bootstrap methods](../concepts/bootstrap-method.md):** Deriving theories from consistency conditions rather than guessing new particles
- **AI-assisted theory:** Could suggest how the 25 particles fit into a larger pattern

### The Resilient View

[Cari Cesarotti](../people/cari-cesarotti.md) (CERN): "Particle physics isn't dead; it's just hard." [Matt Strassler](../people/matt-strassler.md): "It was easy for 125 years. One thing led to the next. That lucky century has, for now, at least in the medium term, come to an end."
