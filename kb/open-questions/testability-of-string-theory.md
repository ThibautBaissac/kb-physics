# Testability of String Theory

The testability of [string theory](../theories/string-theory.md) is one of the most contentious debates in modern physics. Critics argue the theory is "not even wrong" — it cannot be falsified because its predictions lie beyond experimental reach.

## The Two Core Problems

1. **Inaccessible energy scales:** String theory's predicted substructure exists at the Planck scale (~10^19 GeV), far beyond any foreseeable particle accelerator. The [extra dimensions](../concepts/extra-dimensions.md) are compactified at similarly inaccessible scales.

2. **The landscape:** The [string landscape](../concepts/string-landscape.md) of at least 10^500 configurations means the theory can potentially accommodate almost any observation, undermining its predictive power.

## Notable Critics

- **Peter Woit** argues string theory is "not even wrong" — untestable in principle.
- **Sabine Hossenfelder** has called string theory "undead" — neither confirmed nor convincingly killed.

## The Bootstrap Reframing

The [bootstrap approach](../concepts/bootstrap-method.md) shifts the debate. Rather than testing string theory directly, researchers ask whether its foundational assumptions (like [ultrasoftness](../concepts/ultrasoftness.md) or maximal [supersymmetry](../concepts/supersymmetry.md)) are physically reasonable. If string theory is the *unique* theory satisfying certain principles, the question becomes whether those principles hold — a potentially more tractable question.

However, [Astrid Eichhorn](../people/astrid-eichhorn.md) and [Latham Boyle](../people/latham-boyle.md) question whether the [scattering amplitude](../concepts/scattering-amplitudes.md) framework used in these derivations is even valid at the relevant energy scales.

## Current Status

The field remains polarized. Many high-energy physicists privately believe string theory captures some physical truth, while critics maintain it has failed as a scientific theory. The bootstrap results have not resolved this debate but have reframed it around the reasonableness of foundational assumptions rather than direct experimental tests.

## The Broader Experimental Crisis

String theory's testability problem sits within a broader crisis: the [LHC](../experiments/large-hadron-collider.md) found no physics [beyond the Standard Model](../open-questions/beyond-standard-model.md) at all — not just no string-scale physics. This has intensified the debate, as the absence of *any* new experimental guidance makes all theoretical proposals harder to evaluate. Peter Woit, one of string theory's most vocal critics, is quoted in the broader discussion of whether particle physics itself is dying.
